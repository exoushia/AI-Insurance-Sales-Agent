SECTION 1: PRODUCT OVERVIEW
Clause 1.1 – "Swasthya Women Wellness" (SP008), UIN SWIHLIP24008V012024.
Clause 1.2 – Indemnity-based comprehensive health insurance for women aged
    25–55. Standard hospitalisation + women-specific benefits from Day 1.
    Available as Individual policy only.
Clause 1.3 – Covers all standard hospitalisation plus: breast/cervical cancer
    screening (Day 1); HRT coverage; osteoporosis treatment; PCOS/endometriosis
    management; mental health support (₹50,000 OPD sub-limit); annual
    gynaecologist consult; mammography post-40. Target: working women aged 25–55.

SECTION 2: DEFINITIONS
Clause 2.1 – Accident: Sudden, unforeseen external event causing bodily injury.
Clause 2.2 – Cashless Facility (कैशलेस): Direct settlement at Network Hospital.
Clause 2.3 – Claim (दावा): Formal demand for covered expense reimbursement.
Clause 2.4 – Co-payment (सह-भुगतान): Not applicable.
Clause 2.5 – Cancer Screening: Preventive diagnostic tests for early detection
    of breast cancer (mammography, ultrasound, BRCA gene test) and cervical
    cancer (Pap smear, HPV test, colposcopy).
Clause 2.6 – Endometriosis: Chronic gynaecological condition where endometrial
    tissue grows outside the uterus, causing pelvic pain and fertility issues.
Clause 2.7 – HRT: Hormone Replacement Therapy used to manage menopausal symptoms.
Clause 2.8 – Mammography: X-ray imaging of breast tissue for cancer detection.
Clause 2.9 – PCOS: Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome — hormonal disorder common in
    reproductive-age women, causing irregular periods, excess androgen, polycystic ovaries.
Clause 2.10 – Osteoporosis: Condition of reduced bone density increasing fracture risk.
Clause 2.11 – Hospitalisation (अस्पताल में भर्ती): ≥24 hours at registered hospital.
Clause 2.12 – Illness: Sickness or disease requiring medical treatment.
Clause 2.13 – Insured Person: Female policyholder aged 25–55 years.
Clause 2.14 – Network Hospital: Company-empanelled hospital for cashless.
Clause 2.15 – Pre-Existing Disease (पहले से मौजूद बीमारी): Condition diagnosed or
    treated within 48 months prior to first Policy inception.
Clause 2.16 – Premium (प्रीमियम): Annual consideration per Schedule, excl. GST.
Clause 2.17 – Wellness Benefit: Preventive health services included without hospitalisation.
Clause 2.18 – Sum Insured (बीमा राशि): ₹5L or ₹10L per Policy Year.
Clause 2.19 – Waiting Period (प्रतीक्षा अवधि): Duration for which certain conditions
    are not covered from Policy inception.
Clause 2.20 – Mental Health OPD: Outpatient psychiatric/psychological consultations
    and therapy sessions covered under sub-limit.

SECTION 3: ELIGIBILITY AND ENROLLMENT
Clause 3.1 – Entry Age: Females aged 25–55 years only. Not available for males.
Clause 3.2 – Eligible Insured: Individual female. Not available on Family Floater.
Clause 3.3 – Medical Underwriting: No medical exam required below 45 years and
    SI ₹10L. Health declaration required. Above 45 years: Company-designated tests.
Clause 3.4 – Documents: Proposal Form; Aadhaar/PAN; Age proof; Address proof.
Clause 3.5 – Free-Look Period: 15 days from Policy receipt.

SECTION 4: COVERAGE AND BENEFITS
Clause 4.1 – Standard In-patient Hospitalisation: Full indemnity for medically
    necessary in-patient treatment (room, ICU, surgeon, medicines, diagnostics,
    consumables). No room rent sub-limits.

Clause 4.2 – Women-Specific Cancer Coverage (Day 1 — No Waiting Period)
    (a) Breast Cancer: All stages of treatment covered including surgery
        (lumpectomy, mastectomy), chemotherapy, radiotherapy, immunotherapy,
        targeted therapy, hormonal therapy, breast reconstruction surgery.
    (b) Cervical Cancer: Surgery, chemotherapy, radiotherapy, brachytherapy,
        immunotherapy. Covered from Day 1.
    (c) Ovarian Cancer: Surgery, chemotherapy, targeted therapy.
    (d) Uterine/Endometrial Cancer: Surgery, chemotherapy.
    Cancer treatment sub-limit: up to full Sum Insured.

Clause 4.3 – Cancer Screening (Day 1, No Hospitalisation Required)
    Reimbursement for preventive cancer screening annually:
    - Mammography (breast): ₹3,000 per year (post-35; post-30 if family history)
    - Ultrasound (breast): ₹2,000 per year (below 35)
    - Pap smear (cervical): ₹1,500 per year
    - HPV test: ₹2,500 per year
    - Colposcopy (if Pap abnormal): ₹5,000 per event
    - BRCA 1/2 genetic test: ₹20,000 (once per Policy lifetime, if family history)
    Total screening sub-limit: ₹15,000 per Policy Year.

Clause 4.4 – Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT)
    HRT prescribed for menopausal symptoms (hot flushes, vaginal atrophy,
    osteoporosis prevention) covered for pharmacy costs up to ₹12,000 per year
    and in-patient procedures related to HRT monitoring. Must be prescribed by
    registered gynaecologist. Oral, transdermal, and implant forms covered.

Clause 4.5 – Osteoporosis Management
    In-patient hospitalisation for osteoporosis-related fractures covered
    (after 24-month waiting period for non-traumatic osteoporosis; traumatic
    fractures covered from Day 1). OPD bone density scan (DEXA): ₹3,000/year.
    Intravenous bisphosphonate therapy (hospital-administered): covered.
    Oral calcium/vitamin D supplements: not covered (OPD pharmacy).

Clause 4.6 – PCOS/Endometriosis Management
    (a) PCOS: Hospitalisation for PCOS-related procedures (laparoscopic ovarian
        drilling, endometrial biopsy) covered. OPD consultations under mental
        health/wellness benefit only. Hormone therapy for PCOS: ₹8,000/year
        under HRT benefit.
    (b) Endometriosis: Laparoscopic surgery, hysterectomy (if required), hormonal
        treatment (GnRH analogues) for in-patient use. OPD endometriosis
        management: ₹10,000/year sub-limit under wellness benefit.

Clause 4.7 – Mental Health Support (OPD)
    ₹50,000 per Policy Year for outpatient mental health services:
    - Psychiatrist consultations
    - Psychologist/counsellor sessions (up to 12 per year)
    - CBT/DBT therapy sessions
    - Prescribed psychiatric medications (OPD pharmacy with prescription)
    - Online/tele-therapy with licensed professionals
    Mental health inpatient: covered up to full SI (Clause 4.1).

Clause 4.8 – Annual Gynaecologist Consultation
    One complete gynaecological consultation per year at any registered
    gynaecologist: covered up to ₹2,000 (consultation + basic examination).
    Includes annual well-woman check-up with cervical smear and breast examination.

Clause 4.9 – Mammography Post-40
    Annual mammography screening post age 40: ₹4,500 per year (higher-quality
    3D mammography). Note: different from Clause 4.3 standard screening limit.
    Mammography post-40 is a separate dedicated sub-limit.

Clause 4.10 – Maternity (Basic, Optional)
    Optional Maternity Rider (MR-008) available: Normal delivery ₹50,000;
    C-section ₹75,000; pre-natal ₹10,000; post-natal ₹10,000; Newborn 90 days.
    9-month waiting period. Full maternity coverage: refer SP007.

Clause 4.11 – Pre-Hospitalisation: 60 days.
Clause 4.12 – Post-Hospitalisation: 180 days.
Clause 4.13 – Ambulance: ₹2,000 per hospitalisation.
Clause 4.14 – AYUSH (Women's): Ayurvedic treatment for gynaecological
    conditions at registered AYUSH hospitals: up to 50% of SI.
Clause 4.15 – No Claim Bonus: 10% per claim-free year; max 50%.
Clause 4.16 – Day Care: 570 procedures (Annexure I).
Clause 4.17 – Restoration: 100% once per Policy Year for different illness.

Clause 6.1 – Initial: 30 days for illness (not accident; not Day 1 cancer).

Clause 6.3 – PED: 48 months. Note: PCOS, endometriosis diagnosed prior to policy
    may be subject to PED waiting period for hospitalisation; screening benefits
    not restricted by PED waiting period.
Clause 6.4 – Women-Specific Cancer (Day 1): No waiting period. From inception.
Clause 6.5 – Mental Health OPD (Day 1): No waiting period.
Clause 6.6 – Maternity (Rider): 9 months.

SECTION 11: FAQs
Q1. Is breast cancer treatment covered from the first day?
A1. Yes. Swasthya Women Wellness covers all stages of breast cancer treatment
    from Day 1 of the Policy with no waiting period. This includes surgery,
    chemotherapy, radiotherapy, immunotherapy, and reconstruction. This is a
    key differentiator from standard health policies where cancer treatment is
    subject to the standard 30-day initial waiting period.

Q2. क्या PCOS से जुड़ी समस्याएं कवर होती हैं?
    (Are PCOS-related issues covered?)
A2. हाँ। PCOS के लिए अस्पताल में भर्ती होने की प्रक्रियाएं (जैसे लैप्रोस्कोपिक
    ओवेरियन ड्रिलिंग) और हार्मोन थेरेपी कवर हैं। OPD परामर्श ₹10,000/वर्ष
    तक कवर है। हालांकि, यदि PCOS पॉलिसी लेने से पहले से थी, तो 48 महीने की
    PED प्रतीक्षा अवधि लागू होगी।
    (Yes. PCOS hospitalisation procedures and hormone therapy are covered. OPD
    management up to ₹10,000/year. If PCOS is a PED, 48-month wait applies
    for hospitalisation; OPD screening benefits have no PED restriction.)

Q3. Is therapy for work stress or anxiety (not clinical depression) covered?
A3. Yes. The ₹50,000 mental health OPD benefit covers consultations with
    licensed psychologists or counsellors for anxiety, stress, adjustment
    disorders, and mild to moderate depression — not just clinical psychiatric
    disorders. Licensed online therapy platforms (with NIMHANS or RCI-registered
    therapists) are also covered.

Q4. What is the difference between this and adding a maternity rider to SP002?
A4. SP008 is specifically designed for women with Day 1 cancer coverage, cancer
    screening, PCOS management, HRT, and robust mental health OPD. The optional
    maternity rider on SP002 covers delivery only. SP008 with rider MR-008 provides
    both women's health and basic maternity. For comprehensive maternity (including
    IVF), SP007 (Swasthya Maternity Suraksha) is recommended instead.

Q5. Does this cover mammography for a 32-year-old with family history?
A5. Yes. Cancer screening (Clause 4.3) covers mammography from age 30 if there
    is a documented family history of breast cancer, up to ₹3,000/year. BRCA
    1/2 genetic testing (₹20,000, once in Policy lifetime) is also covered with
    documented family history.
