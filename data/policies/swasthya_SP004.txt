SECTION 1: PRODUCT OVERVIEW
================================================================================

Clause 1.1 – Product Name and Identity
"Swasthya Arogya Sanjeevani" (SP004), IRDAI UIN: SWIHLIP24004V012024, is
issued by Swasthya Health Insurance Co. Limited (IRDAI Reg. No. 147).
This product is issued in compliance with the IRDAI (Standard Health
Insurance Product, Arogya Sanjeevani) Guidelines, Circular Ref.
IRDAI/HLT/REG/CIR/194/07/2019, as amended from time to time.

Clause 1.2 – Product Type and Structure
This is the IRDAI-standardised health insurance product. All terms,
conditions, exclusions, and benefits are prescribed by IRDAI and are
uniform across all insurers offering this product. The product name may
include the Company's name prefix but the core product structure cannot
be altered. This Policy is available on Individual and Family Floater basis.

Clause 1.3 – Target Audience and Summary
Swasthya Arogya Sanjeevani is designed for cost-conscious individuals and
families in Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities and rural areas seeking standardised,
affordable hospitalisation cover. The product features a 5% mandatory
co-payment on all admissible claims; room rent capped at 2% of Sum Insured
per day (ICU at 5%); and fixed exclusions per IRDAI circular. No optional
riders are permitted under this product. This standardised product enables
easy comparison across insurers and facilitates portability.

SECTION 2: DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

Clause 2.1 – Accident: Sudden, unforeseen involuntary event by external means.
Clause 2.2 – Cashless Facility (कैशलेस): Direct hospital settlement by Company.
Clause 2.3 – Claim (दावा): Formal demand for payment of covered medical expenses.
Clause 2.4 – Co-payment (सह-भुगतान): 5% of admissible claim borne by Insured
    on every claim. Co-payment does not reduce the Sum Insured.
Clause 2.5 – Deductible (कटौती): No deductible applicable under this product
    (per IRDAI Arogya Sanjeevani guidelines).
Clause 2.6 – Day Care Treatment: Procedures in Annexure I requiring less than

Clause 2.7 – Exclusion (बहिष्करण): Conditions excluded per IRDAI circular
    and Section 7 of this Policy.
Clause 2.8 – Family: Self, Spouse, Children (3 months–25 years, unmarried),
    Parents, Parents-in-Law. Family Floater: max 2 adults + 4 children.
Clause 2.9 – Hospitalisation (अस्पताल में भर्ती): Admission for minimum 24
    consecutive hours, except for listed Day Care Treatments.
Clause 2.10 – Hospital: As per IRDAI standard definition — registered hospital
    with min. 10 beds (smaller towns) or 15 beds (elsewhere), 24-hour nursing
    and medical practitioner, qualified operation theatre.
Clause 2.11 – Illness: Sickness or disease requiring medical treatment.
Clause 2.12 – Insured/Insured Person: Proposer and named members in Policy Schedule.
Clause 2.13 – Network Hospital: Hospital on Company's empanelled cashless list.
Clause 2.14 – Pre-Existing Disease (पहले से मौजूद बीमारी): Condition diagnosed

Clause 2.15 – Premium (प्रीमियम): Annual consideration payable, exclusive of GST.
Clause 2.16 – Sum Insured (बीमा राशि): Maximum aggregate Company liability per

Clause 2.18 – Room Rent Limit: 2% of Sum Insured per day (general ward);
    5% of Sum Insured per day (ICU/ICCU). Exceeding the entitled room category

Clause 2.19 – AYUSH Treatment: Treatment under Ayurveda, Yoga, Naturopathy,
    Unani, Siddha, Homeopathy systems at recognised AYUSH hospitals.
Clause 2.20 – Cataract Sub-Limit: As mandated by IRDAI: 25% of Sum Insured
    or ₹40,000, whichever is lower, per eye.

SECTION 4: COVERAGE AND BENEFITS
================================================================================

Clause 4.1 – In-patient Hospitalisation
Medical expenses for medically necessary in-patient treatment:
(a) Room, boarding, nursing expenses – up to 2% of SI per day;
(b) ICU/ICCU – up to 5% of SI per day;
(c) Surgeon, anaesthetist, consultant, specialist fees;
(d) Anesthesia, blood, oxygen, operation theatre charges;
(e) Medicines, consumables;
(f) Diagnostics, X-ray, laboratory tests.
Note: If actual room rent exceeds 2% SI/day, all associated charges are
proportionately limited to rates applicable at the 2% SI/day room category.

Clause 4.2 – Room Rent Limits (Illustrative)
SI ₹1,00,000: Room ₹2,000/day; ICU ₹5,000/day.
SI ₹2,00,000: Room ₹4,000/day; ICU ₹10,000/day.
SI ₹3,00,000: Room ₹6,000/day; ICU ₹15,000/day.
SI ₹4,00,000: Room ₹8,000/day; ICU ₹20,000/day.
SI ₹5,00,000: Room ₹10,000/day; ICU ₹25,000/day.

Clause 4.3 – Pre-Hospitalisation: 30 days prior to hospitalisation.
Clause 4.4 – Post-Hospitalisation: 60 days from date of discharge.

Clause 4.5 – Day Care Treatments: As listed in Annexure I (IRDAI-approved
    list), including cataract (subject to sub-limit per Clause 2.20).

Clause 4.6 – AYUSH Treatment: In-patient AYUSH treatment up to Sum Insured,
    at IRDAI-recognised AYUSH hospitals. Co-payment of 5% applies.

Clause 4.7 – Road Ambulance: Up to ₹2,000 per hospitalisation.

Clause 4.8 – Mandatory Co-payment
A mandatory co-payment of 5% of the admissible claim amount shall apply
to all claims under this Policy, regardless of age, network/non-network
hospital, or type of illness. The co-payment does not reduce the Sum Insured.
Example: Admissible claim ₹50,000. Co-pay = ₹2,500. Company pays ₹47,500.

Clause 4.9 – No Claim Bonus (NCB)
5% of Base Sum Insured per claim-free year, maximum 50%. (Per IRDAI Arogya
Sanjeevani guidelines, NCB rate is 5%, not the 10% offered in SP001–SP003.)
NCB reduces to nil on any claim in a Policy Year.

Clause 4.10 – Cataract Treatment
Covered after 2-year specific waiting period. Sub-limit: 25% of Sum Insured
or ₹40,000 per eye, whichever is lower. 5% co-pay applies.

Clause 4.11 – Restoration Benefit
Not available under Arogya Sanjeevani. This product does not include
restoration, OPD, consumables cover, or international treatment.

Clause 4.12 – Optional Riders
No optional riders are permitted under this standardised product per
IRDAI guidelines. For additional benefits, a separate policy may be purchased.

SECTION 11: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q1. What is the co-payment under Arogya Sanjeevani and why is it mandatory?
A1. A 5% co-payment on all admissible claims is mandatory under the IRDAI-
    standardised Arogya Sanjeevani product for all insurers. This means if
    your admissible claim is ₹40,000, you pay ₹2,000 and the insurer pays
    ₹38,000. The co-payment encourages responsible healthcare utilisation and
    keeps premiums lower. It does not reduce your Sum Insured.

Q2. क्या मैं Arogya Sanjeevani में कोई अतिरिक्त राइडर जोड़ सकता हूँ?
    (Can I add any additional rider to Arogya Sanjeevani?)
A2. नहीं। IRDAI के नियमानुसार, Arogya Sanjeevani एक मानकीकृत उत्पाद है
    और इसमें कोई भी वैकल्पिक राइडर या अतिरिक्त लाभ नहीं जोड़े जा सकते।
    अतिरिक्त कवरेज के लिए एक अलग पॉलिसी खरीदी जा सकती है।
    (No. Per IRDAI regulations, Arogya Sanjeevani is a standardised product
    and no optional riders can be added. For additional coverage, a separate
    policy can be purchased alongside this one.)

Q3. How is room rent capped and what happens if I take a higher-category room?
A3. Room rent is capped at 2% of Sum Insured per day. If your SI is ₹3 Lakh,
    your entitled room rent is ₹6,000/day. If you occupy a room at ₹10,000/day,
    all associated charges (surgeon fees, nursing, ICU if applicable) will be
    proportionately reduced in the ratio 6,000:10,000 = 60%. You will be
    reimbursed 60% of those charges only.

Q4. Is Arogya Sanjeevani identical across all insurers?
A4. The product structure, benefits, exclusions, and waiting periods are
    standardised and identical across all IRDAI-regulated insurers. The
    premium may vary slightly across insurers based on their actuarial
    assessment, but all key terms are fixed by IRDAI.

Q5. Can I port from another insurer's Arogya Sanjeevani to Swasthya's?
A5. Yes. Portability is straightforward as the product is standardised.
    All accumulated waiting period credits transfer with you. Submit portability
    request to Swasthya at least 45 days before your renewal date.

Q6. Is there any restoration benefit under this plan?
A6. No. The IRDAI-standardised Arogya Sanjeevani does not include a restoration
    benefit. Once the Sum Insured is exhausted in a Policy Year, no further
    claims are payable until renewal. For restoration benefit, consider
    Swasthya Protect (SP001) or Swasthya Protect Plus (SP002).

Q7. What is the maximum Sum Insured under Arogya Sanjeevani?
A7. The maximum Sum Insured under the IRDAI-standardised Arogya Sanjeevani
    product is ₹5,00,000 (Five Lakh). For higher Sum Insured, consider
    Swasthya Protect (SP001: up to ₹10L) or Swasthya Protect Plus (SP002:
    up to ₹50L). A Super Top-Up (SP010) can be added on top.
