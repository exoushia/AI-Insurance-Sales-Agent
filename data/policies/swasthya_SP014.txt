PRODUCT PHILOSOPHY
Cancer treatment in India costs ₹5–35 lakhs or more. Standard health insurance pays
hospitalisation bills but leaves enormous gaps — chemotherapy at home, targeted therapy
drugs, radiation (outpatient), immunotherapy, palliative care, and income loss during
the treatment journey.

SP014 Cancer Protect is a STAGE-BASED BENEFIT policy: it pays differing proportions
of the Sum Insured based on the cancer stage at diagnosis, and continues to pay income
protection benefits through the treatment and recovery period. It is purpose-built for
cancer — the single largest cause of financial catastrophe in Indian families.

CLAUSE 1: DEFINITIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1.1 CANCER OF SPECIFIED SEVERITY: A malignant tumour characterised by uncontrolled
    growth and spread of malignant cells with invasion of tissue, confirmed by
    histopathological examination (biopsy or cytology) and reported by a licensed
    pathologist. Includes: carcinomas, sarcomas, leukaemia, lymphoma, myeloma.

1.2 EARLY-STAGE CANCER (Stage I): Primary cancer localised to the organ of origin,
    no lymph node involvement, no distant metastasis. Includes carcinoma in situ (CIS)
    for this policy's purposes (see Clause 3.1 for CIS benefit level).

1.3 MAJOR CANCER (Stage II–III): Cancer extending beyond the primary site to regional
    lymph nodes but without confirmed distant metastasis.

1.4 ADVANCED CANCER (Stage IV / Metastatic): Cancer with confirmed spread to one or
    more distant organs or tissues.

1.5 CANCER RECURRENCE: Re-appearance of histologically confirmed cancer after a period
    of remission, in the same or different organ.

1.6 SUM INSURED (SI): The base amount against which all stage-based benefits are
    calculated.

1.7 INCOME PROTECTION BENEFIT: Monthly fixed payment during active cancer treatment
    phase, payable in addition to the lump-sum stage benefit.

1.8 REMISSION: Complete absence of cancer as confirmed by imaging and pathology, lasting
    a minimum of 12 consecutive months.

CLAUSE 3: STAGE-BASED BENEFIT SCHEDULE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 FIRST DIAGNOSIS LUMP-SUM PAYOUT:

    Stage at Diagnosis               | % of SI Payable | Survival Period
    ---------------------------------|-----------------|----------------
    Carcinoma in Situ (CIS)          | 25%             | 30 days
    Stage I (Early)                  | 50%             | 30 days
    Stage II–III (Major)             | 100%            | 30 days
    Stage IV (Advanced/Metastatic)   | 100%            | 30 days

    Note: If CIS or Stage I is paid, the remaining SI continues. If 100% is paid
    (Stage II–IV), the policy terminates after the lump sum and income protection
    benefits are fully settled.

3.2 STAGE ESCALATION BENEFIT: If the Insured is first diagnosed at CIS or Stage I
    and the cancer subsequently progresses to Stage II or above within 24 months,
    an additional payment is made:
    — CIS → Stage II–IV: Additional 75% SI (total 100% paid).
    — Stage I → Stage II–IV: Additional 50% SI (total 100% paid).

3.3 CANCER RECURRENCE BENEFIT: After a minimum 12-month remission, if cancer recurs
    (same or different primary), a benefit of 50% SI is payable once. Policy then
    terminates. (This benefit is available only if the original claim was ≤ 50% SI.)

3.4 SECOND PRIMARY CANCER: If a second, unrelated primary cancer is diagnosed after
    remission from the first (certified by oncologist), a benefit of 50% SI is payable.

6.1 ALL HISTOLOGICALLY CONFIRMED CANCERS ARE COVERED, including but not limited to:
    Breast, Lung, Colorectal, Cervical, Ovarian, Uterine, Prostate, Stomach, Liver,
    Pancreatic, Thyroid (except Stage I papillary microcarcinoma < 1 cm, excluded),
    Kidney, Bladder, Brain (primary), Leukaemia, Lymphoma (Hodgkin and Non-Hodgkin),
    Multiple Myeloma, Melanoma, Head and Neck cancers, Oesophageal.

6.2 SPECIFIC EXCLUSIONS FROM COVERAGE:
    — Non-melanoma skin cancers (Basal Cell Carcinoma, Squamous Cell Carcinoma of skin)
    — Kaposi's Sarcoma
    — Papillary Thyroid Microcarcinoma ≤ 1 cm, Stage I
    — Chronic Lymphocytic Leukaemia Grade 1 (Rai Stage 0)
    — Carcinoid tumours of uncertain malignant potential
    — Myelodysplastic syndromes without confirmed leukaemic transformation
