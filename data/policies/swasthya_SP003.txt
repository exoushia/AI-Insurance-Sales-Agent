SECTION 1: PRODUCT OVERVIEW
================================================================================

Clause 1.1 – Product Name and Identity
"Swasthya Protect Global" (SP003), IRDAI UIN: SWIHLIP24003V012024, issued by
Swasthya Health Insurance Co. Limited (IRDAI Reg. No. 147).

Clause 1.2 – Product Type and Structure
This is an indemnity-based premium health insurance policy offering
comprehensive domestic hospitalisation coverage in India combined with
international hospitalisation coverage in partner countries. It is available
on an Individual or Family Floater basis with lifelong renewability.

Clause 1.3 – Target Audience and Summary
Swasthya Protect Global is designed for High-Net-Worth Individuals (HNIs),
senior corporate executives, frequent international travelers, and Non-Resident
Indians (NRIs) who require seamless health coverage across India and abroad.
The Policy features the highest Sum Insured options in the Swasthya Protect
suite, air ambulance coverage, second medical opinion, access to advanced
treatments (robotic surgery, proton therapy, stem cell therapy), and
compassionate travel coverage. Premium is commensurately higher, reflecting
the breadth and depth of coverage offered.

SECTION 2: DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

Clause 2.1 – Accident
"Accident" means a sudden, unforeseen, and involuntary event caused by
external, visible, and violent means resulting in Bodily Injury.

Clause 2.2 – Cashless Facility (कैशलेस)
"Cashless Facility" means direct settlement of hospitalisation costs by
the Company with Network Hospitals or International Partner Hospitals,
subject to pre-authorisation and policy terms.

Clause 2.3 – Claim (दावा)
"Claim" means a formal demand by the Insured or Insured Person for payment
of covered medical expenses under this Policy.

Clause 2.4 – Co-payment (सह-भुगतान)
Co-payment is not applicable under this Policy unless voluntarily selected.

Clause 2.5 – Deductible (कटौती)
No deductible applies unless specifically endorsed on this Policy.

Clause 2.6 – International Partner Hospital
"International Partner Hospital" means a hospital located outside India
that is enrolled in the Company's International Preferred Provider
Organisation (IPPO) network through International SOS, Global Excel
Management, or other approved international TPA partners.

Clause 2.7 – Worldwide Emergency Hospitalisation
"Worldwide Emergency Hospitalisation" means unplanned, emergency In-patient
treatment required outside India due to a sudden, life-threatening condition
or Accidental Bodily Injury, at any registered hospital internationally.

Clause 2.8 – Exclusion (बहिष्करण)
"Exclusion" means any condition or expense for which the Company bears no
liability under this Policy, as specified in Section 7.

Clause 2.9 – Family
"Family" means: Self, legally wedded Spouse, dependent Children aged 91 days
to 25 years, dependent Parents and Parents-in-Law. Maximum 2 adults and
4 children under one Family Floater Policy.

Clause 2.10 – Hospitalisation (अस्पताल में भर्ती)
"Hospitalisation" means admission in a registered Hospital for a minimum of
24 consecutive hours for In-patient Care, except for listed Day Care
Treatments in Annexure I.

Clause 2.11 – Medical Repatriation
"Medical Repatriation" means organised transportation, including via air
ambulance, of an Insured Person from a foreign country to India for
continued medical treatment, following a covered emergency abroad.

Clause 2.12 – Pre-Existing Disease (पहले से मौजूद बीमारी)
"Pre-Existing Disease" means any condition diagnosed or treated within 48
months prior to the first Policy commencement date with the Company.

Clause 2.13 – Premium (प्रीमियम)
"Premium" means the annual consideration payable by the Insured for coverage
under this Policy, as stated in the Policy Schedule, exclusive of GST.

Clause 2.14 – Sum Insured (बीमा राशि)
"Sum Insured" means the maximum aggregate Company liability for all covered
claims during the Policy Year, as stated in the Policy Schedule.

Clause 2.15 – Second Medical Opinion
"Second Medical Opinion" means a formal medical evaluation of the Insured
Person's condition and proposed treatment obtained from a specialist (in
India or abroad) who is not the treating physician.

Clause 2.16 – Waiting Period (प्रतीक्षा अवधि)
"Waiting Period" means the duration from Policy inception during which
specified diseases or treatments are not covered.

Clause 2.17 – Advanced Treatments
"Advanced Treatments" means procedures including robotic-assisted surgery,
proton beam therapy, Gamma Knife radiosurgery, and stem cell therapy for
covered conditions, as defined in IRDAI Circular on Modern/Advanced
Treatments.

Clause 2.18 – Air Ambulance
"Air Ambulance" means a medically equipped aircraft used for emergency
transportation of the Insured Person due to a life-threatening medical
condition, as certified by a qualified Medical Practitioner.

Clause 2.19 – Compassionate Travel
"Compassionate Travel" means emergency travel by an immediate family member
to be with an Insured Person who is hospitalised abroad for more than
5 consecutive days.

Clause 2.20 – No Claim Bonus (NCB)
"No Claim Bonus" means the automatic increase in Base Sum Insured upon
claim-free renewal, as specified in Clause 4.13.

SECTION 4: COVERAGE AND BENEFITS
================================================================================

Clause 4.1 – In-patient Hospitalisation (India)
Full indemnity for all medically necessary In-patient treatment in India:
room/boarding (no sub-limit); ICU/ICCU (no sub-limit); surgeon, anaesthetist,
specialist fees; operation theatre; medicines; consumables; diagnostics;
implants and prosthetics used during surgery.

Clause 4.2 – No Room Rent Sub-Limit
No room rent or ICU restrictions. Single private AC room or suite category
entitled. All associated charges payable at actual rates for chosen room.

Clause 4.3 – Pre-Hospitalisation Expenses
60 days prior to Hospitalisation for the same condition.

Clause 4.4 – Post-Hospitalisation Expenses
180 days from date of discharge for the same condition.

Clause 4.5 – Day Care Treatments
570 procedures listed in Annexure I covered without overnight admission.

Clause 4.6 – Consumables Coverage
All IRDAI non-payable consumables covered as standard benefit.

Clause 4.7 – Domiciliary Hospitalisation
Up to 50% of Sum Insured per Policy Year for conditions requiring
hospitalisation but treated at home under medical supervision.

Clause 4.8 – AYUSH Treatment
Up to 100% of Sum Insured for In-patient AYUSH treatment at recognised
AYUSH hospitals.

Clause 4.9 – OPD Benefit
₹50,000 per Policy Year for outpatient consultations, prescribed diagnostics,
and pharmacy. Available at all SI levels under SP003.

Clause 4.10 – Mental Health Coverage
In-patient psychiatric treatment: up to ₹5,00,000 per Policy Year.
OPD psychiatric consultations: up to ₹25,000 per Policy Year.

Clause 4.11 – Ambulance Charges (India)
Emergency road ambulance: ₹5,000 per Hospitalisation.

Clause 4.12 – Restoration Benefit
Unlimited restoration of 100% Base Sum Insured per Policy Year for different
illnesses. Same terms as SP002 Clause 4.13.

Clause 4.13 – No Claim Bonus (NCB)
10% per claim-free year; maximum 50% of Base SI. NCB applies to domestic
SI only; international SI operates separately.

Clause 4.14 – Maternity Benefit (Rider available)
Optional Maternity Rider MR-003 at ₹50L and above SI. Normal delivery:
₹75,000; C-section: ₹1,00,000; pre-natal: ₹15,000; post-natal: ₹15,000;
Newborn cover Day 1 for 90 days. 9-month waiting period.

Clause 4.15 – Organ Donor Expenses
Fully covered up to available Sum Insured including consumables and
post-operative care.

Clause 4.16 – International Hospitalisation Coverage
Emergency Hospitalisation: Covered worldwide (including sanctioned-country
exceptions per Clause 7.11) at any registered hospital. Pre-planned
elective international treatment: Covered at International Partner
Hospitals (USA, UK, Singapore, Thailand, UAE, Germany, Australia)
subject to minimum 30 days' advance approval.
International Cashless: Available at 12,000+ International Partner
Hospitals via International SOS and Global Excel Management.

Clause 4.17 – Air Ambulance (India and International)
Emergency air ambulance and medical repatriation covered up to ₹10,00,000
per Hospitalisation. Prior approval required (retrospective for emergencies).
Covers: inter-city domestic air ambulance, international air ambulance,
medically equipped ground-to-air transfer.

Clause 4.18 – Second Medical Opinion
Cost of second opinion from recognised specialist in India or abroad:
up to ₹50,000 per Policy Year. Includes tele-consultation with
internationally recognised centres of excellence.

Clause 4.19 – Advanced/Modern Treatments
The following advanced procedures are covered up to available Sum Insured:
(a) Robotic-assisted surgery; (b) Proton beam therapy;
(c) Gamma Knife radiosurgery; (d) Stereotactic radio surgery;
(e) Stem cell therapy for covered haematological conditions;
(f) Gene therapy approved by CDSCO or international equivalent;
(g) Immunotherapy for cancer; (h) Targeted therapy for cancer;
(i) Bone marrow transplant; (j) Intra-vitreal injections;
(k) Balloon sinuplasty; (l) Deep brain stimulation;
(m) Oral chemotherapy drugs (hospitalisation-linked).

Clause 4.20 – Compassionate Travel
If Insured Person is hospitalised abroad for more than 5 consecutive days,
economy class return airfare for one immediate family member covered up to
₹1,00,000. Requires advance intimation to Company.

SECTION 6: WAITING PERIODS
================================================================================

Clause 6.1 – Initial Waiting Period
30 days from inception for illness. Waived on continuous renewal. Accidents
covered from Day 1. International emergency accidents covered from Day 1.

Clause 6.2 – Specific Disease Waiting Periods
(a) 24-month: Cataracts, hernia, hydrocele, fistula, piles, sinusitis,
    benign prostatic hypertrophy, hysterectomy (non-malignant), calculus
    diseases, joint diseases.
(b) 36-month: Joint replacement (non-accidental).
Waiting periods apply to both domestic and international coverage.

Clause 6.3 – Pre-Existing Disease Waiting Period
48 months continuous coverage. Portability credits apply. PED waiting period
applies equally to international hospitalisation claims.

Clause 6.4 – International Coverage Activation
International hospitalisation benefit is active from Policy inception
(Day 1), including for emergency accidental injury abroad. For PED-related
international hospitalisations, the 48-month PED waiting period applies.

SECTION 11: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q1. Is Swasthya Protect Global a travel insurance policy?
A1. No. SP003 is a comprehensive health insurance policy with domestic and
    international hospitalisation benefits. It does not cover trip cancellation,
    baggage loss, flight delays, or travel inconvenience. For travel-specific
    risks, a separate travel insurance policy is recommended.

Q2. Which countries are covered under the international benefit?
A2. Emergency hospitalisation: Worldwide (except sanctioned countries and
    MOFA Level-4 Advisory zones). Planned elective treatment: USA, UK,
    Singapore, Thailand, UAE, Germany, and Australia at International Partner
    Hospitals with 30-day advance approval.

Q3. क्या विदेश में इलाज के लिए कैशलेस सुविधा मिलेगी?
    (Will cashless facility be available for treatment abroad?)
A3. हाँ, 12,000 से अधिक International Partner Hospitals में International SOS
    और Global Excel Management के माध्यम से कैशलेस सुविधा उपलब्ध है। इमरजेंसी
    में तुरंत +91-22-XXXX-XXXX पर कॉल करें।
    (Yes, at 12,000+ International Partner Hospitals through International SOS
    and Global Excel. In emergencies, immediately call +91-22-XXXX-XXXX.)

Q4. Can my family members who are not traveling with me be covered?
A4. Yes, under the Family Floater Policy, all enrolled family members are
    covered for hospitalisation in India throughout the Policy Year. The
    international coverage benefit extends to each enrolled member when they
    travel abroad, subject to policy terms.

Q5. Is proton beam therapy covered only abroad or in India too?
A5. Proton beam therapy is covered under Clause 4.19 both in India (at
    institutions offering this service such as Apollo Proton Cancer Centre,
    Chennai) and internationally at approved centres, subject to available
    Sum Insured and prior Company approval.

Q6. What if I am hospitalised in a country not on the approved list?
A6. Emergency hospitalisation is covered worldwide—any country except those
    under sanctions or MOFA Level-4 advisories. Reimbursement (not cashless)
    applies for hospitalisations at non-partner hospitals. Submit all documents
    within 30 days of return to India.

Q7. How does the air ambulance benefit interact with my Sum Insured?
A7. Air ambulance costs are covered up to ₹10,00,000 per hospitalisation
    and are deducted from the available Sum Insured. If the air ambulance
    cost is ₹8,00,000 and the hospitalisation cost is ₹15,00,000, total
    deduction from SI is ₹23,00,000 (against a SI of ₹50L or higher).
