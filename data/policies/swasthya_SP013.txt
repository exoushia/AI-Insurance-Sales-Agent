PRODUCT PHILOSOPHY
A critical illness is a financial event as much as a medical one. SP013 pays a TAX-FREE
LUMP SUM upon diagnosis of a covered critical illness — no bills, no hospitalisation
required. The payout may be used for: treatment costs, experimental therapies abroad,
loan repayment, income replacement during recovery, or family relocation. The Insured
decides how the money is used.

Hindi: "गंभीर बीमारी बीमा" (Gambhir Bimari Bima) — निदान (Diagnosis) पर एकमुश्त (Ek Musht)
भुगतान — इलाज या जरूरत के अनुसार।

This is a FIXED BENEFIT policy. Payouts are not linked to actual medical expenses incurred.

CLAUSE 1: CRITICAL ILLNESS LIST (20 CONDITIONS COVERED)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Category I — 100% of Sum Insured (Tier A conditions):

  1.  CANCER OF SPECIFIED SEVERITY: Malignant tumour characterised by uncontrolled
      growth and spread of malignant cells with invasion of tissue. Includes leukaemia,
      lymphoma, and sarcoma. Excludes: carcinoma in situ (CIS); non-melanoma skin
      cancers; Kaposi's sarcoma; CLL Grade 1.

  2.  OPEN-HEART REPLACEMENT OR REPAIR OF HEART VALVES: Open cardiac surgery to replace
      or repair one or more heart valves; must result in a sternotomy.

  3.  CORONARY ARTERY BYPASS GRAFTING (CABG): Surgical bypass grafting of two or more
      coronary arteries; angioplasty/stenting alone does NOT qualify.

  4.  FIRST HEART ATTACK OF SPECIFIED SEVERITY (MYOCARDIAL INFARCTION): Evidence of
      current cardiac failure, ECG changes, and troponin elevation; must meet ESC criteria.

  5.  STROKE RESULTING IN PERMANENT SYMPTOMS: Infarction or haemorrhage of brain tissue
      causing neurological deficit persisting ≥ 90 days from onset.

  6.  KIDNEY FAILURE REQUIRING REGULAR DIALYSIS: End-stage renal failure requiring
      permanent, regular dialysis or renal transplant.

  7.  MAJOR ORGAN / BONE MARROW TRANSPLANT: Transplant of heart, lung, liver, kidney,
      pancreas, or bone marrow from a human donor.

  8.  PERMANENT PARALYSIS OF LIMBS: Total and irreversible loss of use of at least 2
      limbs for a minimum of 3 months following accident or illness.

  9.  MULTIPLE SCLEROSIS WITH PERSISTING SYMPTOMS: Definitive diagnosis by neurologist;
      symptoms persisting ≥ 6 months.

  10. MOTOR NEURONE DISEASE WITH PERMANENT SYMPTOMS: ALS, PBP, PMA, PLS — progressive
      neurodegeneration confirmed; permanent neurological symptoms.

Category II — 50% of Sum Insured (Tier B conditions):

  11. ANGIOPLASTY / CORONARY STENTING: Minimum of 2-vessel PTCA/stenting for ≥ 70%
      occlusion as confirmed by angiography.

  12. AORTA GRAFT SURGERY: Surgical repair of aortic aneurysm or dissection requiring
      cardiopulmonary bypass.

  13. PRIMARY PULMONARY ARTERIAL HYPERTENSION: Mean PAP > 25 mmHg at rest; Grade III
      or IV WHO functional classification.

  14. COMA OF SPECIFIED SEVERITY: GCS ≤ 6, medically induced coma excluded; must
      persist ≥ 96 continuous hours with no purposeful response.

  15. LOSS OF SIGHT (PERMANENT AND IRRECOVERABLE): Both eyes; corrected visual acuity
      ≤ 3/60 in better eye.

  16. LOSS OF HEARING (PERMANENT): Bilateral; >90 dB in better ear on audiometry.

  17. LOSS OF SPEECH (PERMANENT): Total and irrecoverable loss; must persist ≥ 6 months.

  18. SEVERE BURNS: Third-degree burns covering ≥ 20% of total body surface area.

  19. BACTERIAL MENINGITIS RESULTING IN PERMANENT SYMPTOMS: Confirmed by CSF culture;
      permanent neurological deficit persisting ≥ 90 days.

  20. APLASTIC ANAEMIA: Permanent bone marrow failure; requires blood/platelet transfusions;
      Haemoglobin < 10 g/dL; neutrophils < 0.5 × 10⁹/L.

CLAUSE 3: SURVIVAL PERIOD AND CLAIM TRIGGER
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 SURVIVAL PERIOD: The Insured must survive for a minimum of 30 days following
    the date of first confirmed diagnosis (or date of qualifying surgery, for conditions
    such as CABG or valve replacement).

3.2 DIAGNOSIS MUST BE: Made by a medical specialist (Oncologist, Cardiologist,
    Neurologist, Nephrologist, as appropriate) in India or in a recognised institution
    abroad, supported by laboratory/imaging/histopathology reports.

3.3 FIRST DIAGNOSIS REQUIREMENT: Benefit is payable only upon first diagnosis of a
    covered condition in a person who was free of that condition at inception and
    throughout all waiting periods.

3.4 POLICY TERMINATES AFTER CLAIM: Upon payment of 100% Sum Insured (Tier A), the
    policy terminates. Upon payment of 50% Sum Insured (Tier B), the policy continues
    with the remaining 50% SI available for a Tier A claim only.

CLAUSE 4: WAITING PERIODS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4.1 INITIAL WAITING PERIOD: 90 days from inception.
    All critical illness diagnoses within the first 90 days are excluded.
    EXCEPTION: Accidents (e.g., stroke from trauma, severe burns from fire) — Day 1.

4.2 PRE-EXISTING CONDITIONS: Any critical illness for which symptoms existed, or
    treatment was received, or which was investigated in the 48 months prior to
    inception is excluded.

4.3 SPECIFIC WAITING PERIODS:
    — Cancer (all types): 90 days initial wait + PED exclusion
    — Cardiac conditions: 90 days initial wait
    — Coma, paralysis, burns: Day 1 if accident-caused; 90 days if disease-caused
