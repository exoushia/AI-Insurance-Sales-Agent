SECTION 1: PRODUCT OVERVIEW
================================================================================

Clause 1.1 – Product Name and Identity
This Policy is issued under the product name "Swasthya Protect" (hereinafter
referred to as "the Policy" or "SP001"). It is a health indemnity insurance
product offered by Swasthya Health Insurance Co. Limited (hereinafter referred
to as "the Company," "We," "Us," or "Our"), registered with the Insurance
Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI), bearing Registration
No. 147. The Unique Identification Number (UIN) assigned by IRDAI for this
product is SWIHLIP24001V012024.

Clause 1.2 – Product Type and Structure
Swasthya Protect is an indemnity-based health insurance policy available on
both Individual and Family Floater basis. Under the Individual basis, the Sum
Insured (बीमा राशि) is available exclusively to the named Insured Person.
Under the Family Floater basis, the Sum Insured is shared among all enrolled
family members in aggregate during the Policy Year. This Policy is renewable
on an annual basis and confers lifelong renewability subject to the terms
and conditions herein.

Clause 1.3 – Target Audience and Summary
Swasthya Protect is designed for first-time health insurance buyers, young
professionals, and nuclear families seeking affordable, foundational
hospitalisation coverage in India. The Policy provides coverage against
reasonable and customary medical expenses arising from Illness or Accidental
Bodily Injury requiring Hospitalisation. Room rent sub-limits apply at the
₹3 Lakh and ₹5 Lakh Sum Insured tiers. A basic Restoration Benefit is
included. The Policy prioritises simplicity, affordability, and accessibility
while complying fully with IRDAI (Health Insurance) Regulations, 2016 and
all applicable IRDAI circulars.

SECTION 2: DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

Clause 2.1 – Accident
"Accident" means a sudden, unforeseen, and involuntary event caused by
external, visible, and violent means, resulting in Bodily Injury to the
Insured Person.

Clause 2.2 – Cashless Facility (कैशलेस)
"Cashless Facility" means a facility extended by the Company, through its
Third Party Administrator (TPA), to the Insured Person, whereby costs of
treatment undergone at a Network Hospital are directly settled by the Company
with the hospital to the extent of pre-authorisation approval, subject to
policy terms and applicable sub-limits.

Clause 2.3 – Claim (दावा)
"Claim" means a formal request made by the Insured or Insured Person to the
Company for payment of benefits arising from a covered event under this Policy.

Clause 2.4 – Co-payment (सह-भुगतान)
"Co-payment" means a cost-sharing requirement whereby the Insured Person
bears a specified percentage of the admissible claim amount. Co-payment is
not applicable under the standard Swasthya Protect plan unless selected as
an optional feature at inception.

Clause 2.5 – Deductible (कटौती)
"Deductible" means a fixed amount which the Insured Person shall bear from
each admissible claim before the Company's liability commences. No deductible
applies under this Policy unless specifically endorsed.

Clause 2.6 – Day Care Treatment
"Day Care Treatment" means medical treatment or a surgical procedure that,
owing to technological advancement, is undertaken under general or local
anaesthesia in a Hospital or Day Care Centre in less than 24 consecutive
hours and which would have otherwise required Hospitalisation for more than
24 hours. Treatment normally taken on an outpatient basis is not included.

Clause 2.7 – Exclusion (बहिष्करण)
"Exclusion" means any condition, disease, treatment, circumstance, or expense
expressly listed in Section 7 of this Policy for which the Company shall not
be liable to make any payment, whether in whole or in part.

Clause 2.8 – Family
"Family" means the Insured, his/her legally wedded Spouse, dependent
Children (natural, adopted, or step-children) between 91 days and 25 years
of age, and dependent Parents and Parents-in-Law. A maximum of two adults and
four children may be enrolled under a single Family Floater Policy.

Clause 2.9 – Hospitalisation (अस्पताल में भर्ती)
"Hospitalisation" means admission in a Hospital for a minimum period of
24 consecutive hours for In-patient Care, except for specified Day Care
Treatments as listed in Annexure I, where such admission may be for a
period of less than 24 consecutive hours.

Clause 2.10 – Hospital
"Hospital" means any institution established for In-patient Care and Day
Care Treatment of illness and/or injuries that has been registered as a
Hospital with the local authorities under the Clinical Establishments
(Registration and Regulation) Act, 2010, or complies with all minimum
criteria as defined under IRDAI regulations, including: at least 10 in-patient
beds in towns with population below 10,00,000 and at least 15 in-patient beds
elsewhere; qualified nursing staff round the clock; qualified Medical
Practitioner(s) in charge round the clock; a fully equipped operation theatre;
and maintenance of daily patient records.

Clause 2.11 – Illness
"Illness" means a sickness, disease, or pathological condition leading to
impairment of normal physiological function and requiring medical treatment.
This includes acute conditions (likely to respond quickly to treatment) and
chronic conditions (requiring long-term management).

Clause 2.12 – Insured / Insured Person
"Insured" means the Proposer named in the Policy Schedule who has entered
into this insurance contract with the Company. "Insured Person" means any
person named in the Policy Schedule for whom insurance cover has been
accepted and premium received by the Company.

Clause 2.13 – Network Hospital
"Network Hospital" means a Hospital empanelled by the Company or its TPA to
provide cashless treatment services to Insured Persons, as updated from time
to time. The current list is available at www.swasthyainsurance.in and with
the TPA's 24-hour helpline.

Clause 2.14 – No Claim Bonus (NCB)
"No Claim Bonus" means the cumulative increase in Sum Insured (बीमा राशि)
granted by the Company upon renewal of the Policy without any admissible
claim having been made or paid during the preceding Policy Year.

Clause 2.15 – Pre-Existing Disease (पहले से मौजूद बीमारी)
"Pre-Existing Disease" means any condition, ailment, injury, or disease
that is/was diagnosed by a physician within 48 months prior to the issuance
of the first Policy by the Company; or for which medical advice or treatment
was recommended by, or received from, a physician within 48 months prior to
the date of commencement of the first Policy.

Clause 2.16 – Premium (प्रीमियम)
"Premium" means the amount payable by the Insured to the Company for the
insurance cover provided under this Policy for the Policy Year, as specified
in the Policy Schedule, exclusive of applicable Goods and Services Tax (GST)
and other statutory levies.

Clause 2.17 – Sum Insured (बीमा राशि)
"Sum Insured" means the maximum aggregate liability of the Company in respect
of all admissible claims during a Policy Year, as specified in the Policy
Schedule. Under a Family Floater Policy, the Sum Insured is the combined
maximum for all Insured Persons collectively.

Clause 2.18 – Third Party Administrator (TPA)
"TPA" means a Company registered with IRDAI engaged by the Company to provide
claims facilitation and health management services to Insured Persons.

Clause 2.19 – Waiting Period (प्रतीक्षा अवधि)
"Waiting Period" means a period from the inception of this Policy during which
specified diseases and/or treatments are not covered by the Company. Upon
completion of the applicable Waiting Period, coverage commences subject to
continuous and uninterrupted renewal of the Policy.

Clause 2.20 – Restoration Benefit
"Restoration Benefit" means the reinstatement of the Sum Insured by the
Company, once fully exhausted during a Policy Year, as specified in Clause 4.12.

Clause 3.1 – Entry Age
The minimum entry age for the Insured (Proposer) and adult Insured Persons
is 18 years. The maximum entry age is 65 years. Dependent children may be
enrolled from a minimum age of 91 days. Dependent children remain covered
under a Family Floater Policy until they attain the age of 25 years or
become financially independent, whichever is earlier.

Clause 3.2 – Eligible Relationships
The following relationships are eligible for enrollment under a single Policy:
(a) Self (Insured/Proposer); (b) Legally wedded Spouse; (c) Dependent
Children (natural, adopted, or legally recognised step-children aged 91 days
to 25 years); (d) Parents (biological or legally adopted); (e) Parents-in-Law.
A maximum of two adults and up to four dependent children are permitted under
one Family Floater Policy. Parents and Parents-in-Law may be added as
separate members on a Family Floater basis subject to applicable premium.

Clause 3.3 – Medical Examination
Proposal for insurance up to 45 years of age and Sum Insured up to ₹10
Lakh does not require a pre-acceptance medical examination, subject to
satisfactory completion of the Health Declaration Form. Persons above 45
years of age or seeking Sum Insured above ₹10 Lakh shall be required to
undergo medical examination at Company-empanelled diagnostic centres, the
cost of which shall be borne by the Company if the Proposal is accepted.

Clause 3.4 – Enrollment Process
The Proposer shall submit a duly completed Proposal Form (available at
www.swasthyainsurance.in or through licensed intermediaries), accompanied
by the following documents: (a) Self-attested copy of Proof of Identity
(Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Passport, or Voter ID); (b) Self-attested copy of
Proof of Age; (c) Self-attested copy of Proof of Address; (d) Completed
Health Declaration Form; and (e) Recent passport-size photographs.
The Policy shall be issued within 48 hours of receipt of complete documentation
and realisation of premium.

Clause 3.5 – Free-Look Period
The Insured shall be entitled to a free-look period of 15 days from the date
of receipt of the Policy document to review the terms and conditions. If the
Insured finds the terms unacceptable, the Policy may be returned with written
notice to the Company for cancellation and full premium refund, less stamp
duty charges and proportionate premium for the period of cover, provided no
claim has been made or is pending.

SECTION 4: COVERAGE AND BENEFITS
================================================================================

Clause 4.1 – In-patient Hospitalisation
The Company shall indemnify the Insured Person for Reasonable and Customary
Medical Expenses incurred during Hospitalisation for medically necessary
treatment of an Illness or Accidental Bodily Injury, including:
(a) Room, boarding, and nursing charges; (b) ICU/ICCU charges;
(c) Medical Practitioner's, Surgeon's, and Specialist's fees;
(d) Anesthesia, blood, oxygen, operation theatre charges;
(e) Medicines, drugs, and consumables; (f) Diagnostic procedures and tests;
(g) Cost of prosthetic implants if used during surgery.

Clause 4.2 – Room Rent Sub-Limits
Room rent limits apply as follows:
- Sum Insured ₹3 Lakh: Room Rent limited to ₹2,500 per day; ICU up to ₹5,000
  per day. In case the Insured Person occupies a room with rent exceeding the
  applicable limit, all associated charges (surgeon fees, specialist fees,
  nursing charges) shall be proportionately reduced.
- Sum Insured ₹5 Lakh: Room Rent limited to ₹4,000 per day; ICU up to ₹8,000
  per day. Proportionate deduction applies as above.
- Sum Insured ₹10 Lakh: No room rent sub-limit. Actual charges payable subject
  to Reasonable and Customary Charges.

Clause 4.3 – Pre-Hospitalisation Expenses
Medical expenses incurred up to 60 days prior to the date of Hospitalisation
for the same condition for which Hospitalisation was required shall be covered,
subject to the admissibility of the In-patient claim.

Clause 4.4 – Post-Hospitalisation Expenses
Medical expenses incurred up to 90 days following the date of discharge from
Hospital for the same condition for which Hospitalisation was undertaken shall
be covered, subject to the admissibility of the In-patient claim.

Clause 4.5 – Day Care Treatments
The Policy covers Day Care Treatments as listed in Annexure I (comprising 540
procedures), including but not limited to: cataract surgery, chemotherapy,
radiotherapy, lithotripsy, dialysis, coronary angiography, and laparoscopic
cholecystectomy. Day Care claims do not require overnight Hospitalisation.

Clause 4.6 – Domiciliary Hospitalisation
Treatment taken at the Insured Person's residence for a condition that would
have ordinarily required Hospitalisation, where Hospitalisation was not
undertaken due to the patient's inability to be moved or lack of hospital
accommodation, shall be covered for a maximum period of 15 days per illness
episode, subject to a sub-limit of ₹50,000 per Policy Year.

Clause 4.7 – Ambulance Charges
Emergency road ambulance charges shall be covered up to ₹2,000 per
Hospitalisation, for transportation to the nearest appropriate Hospital.
Air ambulance is not covered under this plan; refer to SP003 for air
ambulance coverage.

Clause 4.8 – AYUSH Treatment
In-patient treatment under Ayurveda, Yoga and Naturopathy, Unani, Siddha,
or Homeopathy (AYUSH) systems of medicine shall be covered up to 25% of the
Sum Insured per Policy Year, provided treatment is undertaken at a recognised
AYUSH Hospital as defined under applicable IRDAI guidelines.

Clause 4.9 – Organ Donor Expenses
Medical expenses incurred for harvesting an organ from a donor for transplant
to the Insured Person shall be covered up to the available Sum Insured,
subject to the Company accepting the Insured Person's In-patient claim for
the transplant procedure.

Clause 4.10 – Cataract Treatment
Cataract treatment shall be covered after completion of the 24-month specific
waiting period as specified in Clause 6.2(b), subject to a sub-limit of
₹40,000 per eye per Policy Year.

Clause 4.11 – Mental Health Coverage
In-patient psychiatric treatment shall be covered on par with physical illness,
as mandated by IRDAI Circular No. IRDAI/HLT/REG/CIR/044/02/2020, subject to
a sub-limit of ₹1,50,000 per Policy Year. OPD psychiatric consultations are
not covered under this plan.

Clause 4.12 – Restoration Benefit
Upon exhaustion of the entire Sum Insured (including NCB, if any) during a
Policy Year due to admissible claims, the Company shall restore 100% of the
Base Sum Insured once per Policy Year. The Restored Sum Insured shall be
available for Hospitalisation arising from any illness or injury different
from that for which the Sum Insured was exhausted. The Restored Sum Insured
shall not be carried forward to the next Policy Year and cannot be utilised
for the same illness/injury that caused exhaustion.

Clause 4.13 – No Claim Bonus (NCB)
Upon renewal of the Policy without any admissible claim during the preceding
Policy Year, the Company shall increase the Sum Insured by 10% of the Base
Sum Insured at no additional premium, subject to a maximum cumulative NCB
of 50% of the Base Sum Insured. In the event of a claim during any Policy
Year, the accumulated NCB shall reduce by 10% at the time of renewal.
NCB does not apply to the Restored Sum Insured.

Clause 4.14 – Consumables Coverage
Standard non-payable consumables as per IRDAI's Gazette Notification dated
14 July 2020 (circular reference IRDAI/HLT/CIR/MISC/151/08/2019) shall not
be covered under this Policy. Medically implanted devices and prosthetics
used during a covered surgical procedure shall be covered.

Clause 4.15 – Maternity and Newborn Coverage
Maternity coverage is not included as a standard benefit under this Policy.
A Maternity Rider (MR-001) is available as an optional add-on, subject to
separate terms, conditions, and a 12-month waiting period from rider
inception. Refer to the Maternity Rider endorsement document for details.

SECTION 6: WAITING PERIODS
================================================================================

Clause 6.1 – Initial Waiting Period
No claim shall be payable for any illness (other than those arising from
Accidental Bodily Injury) occurring within 30 days of the Policy commencement
date for a new Policy. This waiting period shall not apply upon continuous
and uninterrupted renewal of the Policy. Claims arising from Accidental
Bodily Injury are covered from the Policy commencement date.

Clause 6.2 – Specific Disease Waiting Periods
The following conditions and treatments shall not be covered during the
initial periods specified below from the Policy commencement date:
(a) 24-month waiting period: Cataracts; benign prostatic hypertrophy;
    hysterectomy for non-malignant conditions; all types of hernia; hydrocele;
    fistula-in-ano and piles (haemorrhoids); sinusitis and related disorders;
    calculus diseases (kidney, gall bladder, urinary tract); non-infective
    arthritis; treatment for joint replacement due to degenerative conditions
    (36-month period for joint replacement).
(b) 36-month waiting period: Joint replacement surgery (unless necessitated
    by Accidental Bodily Injury).
These waiting periods shall be progressively reduced upon portability from
another insurer in accordance with IRDAI portability guidelines.

Clause 6.3 – Pre-Existing Disease (PED) Waiting Period
Any Pre-Existing Disease (पहले से मौजूद बीमारी) as defined in Clause 2.15
shall not be covered until the completion of 48 consecutive months of
continuous coverage from the date of inception of the first Policy issued
by the Company covering such Pre-Existing Disease. Portability credits shall
apply as per IRDAI guidelines.

Clause 6.4 – Maternity Waiting Period
Maternity coverage under the optional Maternity Rider (MR-001) is subject
to a 12-month waiting period from the date of rider inception. No waiting
period applies for treatment of life-threatening complications of pregnancy.

SECTION 7: EXCLUSIONS
================================================================================

The Company shall not be liable to make any payment under this Policy in
respect of expenses directly or indirectly incurred in connection with or
arising out of the following:

Clause 7.1 – Pre-Existing Diseases During Waiting Period
Any Pre-Existing Disease and its direct complications during the applicable
waiting period as specified in Clause 6.3.

Clause 7.2 – Initial Waiting Period Illnesses
Any illness contracted within the first 30 days of the Policy commencement
date, except Accidental Bodily Injury as per Clause 6.1.

Clause 7.3 – Cosmetic and Aesthetic Treatments
Any treatment, surgical or otherwise, undertaken solely for cosmetic,
aesthetic, or beauty enhancement purposes, including but not limited to
rhinoplasty, liposuction, breast augmentation, and skin lightening procedures,
unless necessitated by Accidental Bodily Injury or Cancer treatment.

Clause 7.4 – Dental Treatment
Dental treatment or surgery of any kind, including tooth extraction, dental
implants, orthodontic treatment, and periodontal surgery, unless necessitated
by Accidental Bodily Injury to natural teeth requiring Hospitalisation.

Clause 7.5 – Self-Inflicted Injury
Treatment arising from intentional self-inflicted injury, attempted suicide,
or deliberate self-harm, irrespective of the mental health status of the
Insured Person at the time of the act.

Clause 7.6 – Substance Abuse and Addiction
Treatment for alcoholism, drug addiction, substance abuse, or any condition
directly arising from chronic use of alcohol, narcotics, tobacco, or
psychoactive substances. De-addiction treatment is excluded.

Clause 7.7 – War, Terrorism, and Nuclear Risks
Any claim arising directly or indirectly from war (declared or undeclared),
invasion, acts of foreign enemies, civil war, rebellion, revolution,
insurrection, military or usurped power, nuclear contamination, radioactive
fallout, chemical warfare, or biological attack.

Clause 7.8 – Maternity and Reproductive Treatments (Standard Exclusion)
Normal pregnancy and childbirth, miscarriage, abortion, or any complications
thereof, unless covered under the optional Maternity Rider (MR-001).
Assisted reproductive technologies (IVF, IUI, ICSI, GIFT, ZIFT) and
gestational surrogacy are excluded under the standard Policy.

Clause 7.9 – Infertility and Sterility
Treatment for infertility, sub-fertility, impotence, or sexual dysfunction,
including but not limited to hormonal therapy for fertility purposes and
surgical correction for infertility.

Clause 7.10 – Experimental and Unproven Treatments
Any treatment, procedure, or technology that lacks established medical
evidence of efficacy as per the standards of the medical community in India
or internationally recognised medical practice, including experimental drugs
not approved by the Central Drugs Standard Control Organisation (CDSCO).

Clause 7.11 – Congenital Anomalies
Treatment for any External Congenital Anomaly (conditions present since birth
that are visible and accessible). Internal Congenital Anomalies are covered
after completion of the applicable waiting period under Clause 6.3.

Clause 7.12 – Treatments Abroad
Any medical treatment undertaken outside the geographical territory of India.
Refer to SP003 (Swasthya Protect Global) for international coverage.

Clause 7.13 – OPD and Non-Hospitalisation Treatments
Expenses incurred on outpatient consultations, OPD diagnostics, pharmacy
purchases for non-hospitalised conditions, and home nursing care, unless
specifically covered under Clause 4.6 (Domiciliary Hospitalisation).

Clause 7.14 – Preventive Care and Vaccinations
Health check-ups, preventive medical examinations, vaccinations, inoculations,
and immunisations, except where expressly included as a covered benefit.

Clause 7.15 – Change of Gender Treatment
Any treatment, surgical or medical, for the purpose of gender reassignment
or sexual transformation.

Clause 7.16 – Refractive Errors
Expenses for treatment of refractive errors of the eye (spectacles, contact
lenses, laser refractive surgery such as LASIK/LASEK) unless the refractive
error exceeds 7.5 dioptres.

Clause 7.17 – Non-Medical Expenses
Expenses of a personal nature incurred during Hospitalisation, including but
not limited to attendant charges, television rental, telephone charges,
laundry, personal hygiene items, food for family members, and administrative
charges, as per IRDAI's non-payables list (Gazette Notification 2020).

Clause 7.18 – Obesity and Weight Management
Treatment for obesity, morbid obesity, weight reduction, or weight management,
including bariatric surgery, unless the patient meets all criteria specified
in IRDAI guidelines for medically necessary obesity surgery.

Clause 7.19 – Hearing Aids and External Devices
Cost of hearing aids, spectacles, contact lenses, crutches, wheelchairs,
artificial limbs, dentures, artificial teeth, and other external aids or
appliances, unless used as part of a covered surgical procedure.

Clause 7.20 – Epidemics and Government-Declared Disasters
Treatment for any epidemic, pandemic, or public health emergency as declared
by the World Health Organisation (WHO) or the Government of India, unless
specifically covered by an endorsement or IRDAI direction.

Clause 7.21 – Adventure Sports and Hazardous Activities
Treatment necessitated by participation as a professional in adventure sports
or hazardous activities, including but not limited to bungee jumping, skydiving,
mountaineering, motor racing, or deep-sea diving.

Clause 7.22 – Circumcision
Circumcision, unless medically necessary for the treatment of a covered
illness or as a result of Accidental Bodily Injury.

Clause 7.23 – Excluded Hospitals and Providers
Treatment at any hospital or by any medical practitioner specifically excluded
by the Company and notified on the Company's website, except in life-threatening
emergencies where expenses up to the point of stabilisation shall be payable.

Clause 7.24 – Genetic Testing and Treatment
Genetic testing, counselling, or treatment arising from genetic or hereditary
conditions, including prophylactic surgery based on genetic predisposition.

Clause 7.25 – Domiciliary Hospitalisation Specific Exclusions
Under Clause 4.6, Domiciliary Hospitalisation shall not cover: pre- and
post-domiciliary expenses; treatment for asthma, bronchitis, chronic nephritis,
diarrhoea, diabetes mellitus and insipidus, epilepsy, hypertension, influenza,
all psychiatric or psychosomatic disorders, tonsillitis, and arthritis/gout/
rheumatism during domiciliary treatment.

SECTION 8: CLAIM PROCEDURE
================================================================================

Clause 8.1 – General Conditions Precedent
The due observance and fulfilment of the terms and conditions of this Policy
by the Insured and Insured Person, including timely notification of claims,
submission of complete documentation, and co-operation with the Company's
investigation, shall be conditions precedent to any liability of the Company
under this Policy.

Clause 8.2 – Cashless Claim Procedure (Network Hospitals)
(a) For planned Hospitalisation: The Insured Person or hospital shall intimate
    the Company's TPA at least 48 hours prior to admission through the
    Cashless Desk at the Network Hospital or by calling the 24-hour TPA
    helpline (1800-XXX-XXXX, toll-free).
(b) For emergency Hospitalisation: Intimation shall be provided within
    6 hours of admission.
(c) The TPA shall issue initial pre-authorisation within 1 hour of receipt
    of complete pre-authorisation request form.
(d) Final pre-authorisation (enhancement, if required) shall be issued
    within 6 hours of receipt of request during Hospitalisation.
(e) Documents required for pre-authorisation: Completed pre-authorisation
    form (available at Network Hospital); copy of health card/policy number;
    treating doctor's certificate of medical necessity; preliminary diagnosis
    and proposed treatment plan; KYC documents of Insured Person.
(f) The Company or TPA may deny or partially approve Cashless Facility for
    non-covered conditions. The Insured Person may proceed with treatment and
    file a reimbursement claim.

Clause 8.3 – Reimbursement Claim Procedure (Non-Network Hospitals and
             Post-Cashless Denied Claims)
(a) The Insured Person shall intimate the claim to the Company/TPA within
    48 hours of Hospitalisation (24 hours for emergency).
(b) Claim documents shall be submitted to the TPA within 15 days of
    discharge from Hospital.
(c) For post-hospitalisation claims, documents shall be submitted within
    15 days of completion of post-hospitalisation treatment, subject to a
    maximum of 75 days from the date of discharge.
(d) The Company shall settle or reject the claim within 30 days of receipt
    of complete documentation. In case of investigation, settlement shall
    occur within 45 days.

Clause 8.4 – Mandatory Claim Documents
The following documents must be submitted with every claim:
(i) Duly completed and signed Claim Form (Part A: Insured's Declaration;
    Part B: Doctor's Certificate);
(ii) Original/attested copies of hospital bills and receipts (itemised);
(iii) Original discharge summary (detailed, signed by treating doctor);
(iv) Original investigation reports, X-rays, scans, ECG, biopsy reports;
(v) Original pharmacy bills with prescriptions;
(vi) Attending doctor's prescription advising hospitalisation;
(vii) Copy of FIR/MLC (for accidental claims);
(viii) Copy of Policy document and health card;
(ix) Cancelled cheque with pre-printed account name (for NEFT);
(x) CKYC number or duly completed KYC form.

Clause 8.5 – Additional Documents (if required by TPA)
(i) Indoor case papers (nurse's notes, OT notes, anaesthetist's notes);
(ii) Pre-authorisation correspondence;
(iii) Certificate from previous insurer (for portability claims);
(iv) Legal heir certificate (for claims on deceased policyholder).

Clause 8.6 – Claim Settlement Timeline
The Company shall settle valid claims within 30 days of receipt of all
required documents. In the event of delay beyond 30 days, interest at 2%
per annum above the prevailing bank rate of the Reserve Bank of India (RBI)
shall be payable on the claim amount from the date of receipt of last
document to the date of actual payment.

Clause 8.7 – Claim Investigation
Where circumstances warrant investigation, the Company shall initiate and
complete investigation within 30 days of claim intimation. If investigation
cannot be completed within 30 days, the Company shall communicate the
reasons in writing to the Insured.

Clause 8.8 – Penal Provisions for Fraudulent Claims
If any claim is found to be fraudulent, all benefits under the Policy shall
be forfeited and the Company shall have the right to cancel the Policy and
recover any amounts already paid. The Company shall report such cases to
the Insurance Information Bureau of India (IIB) as per applicable regulations.

SECTION 9: RENEWAL, PORTABILITY, AND CANCELLATION
================================================================================

Clause 9.1 – Renewal
This Policy is renewable annually on a lifelong basis. The Company shall not
decline renewal on the basis of claims history, health deterioration, or any
change in risk profile, except in cases of fraud, moral hazard, or established
misrepresentation. The Company shall provide renewal notice at least 45 days
before the Policy expiry date.

Clause 9.2 – Grace Period
A grace period of 30 days from the Policy expiry date is available for premium
payment without loss of continuity benefits (NCB, waiting period credits). No
coverage shall be available for the period of the lapse for which no premium
has been received.

Clause 9.3 – Portability
The Insured Person has the right to port this Policy to another insurer
in accordance with IRDAI (Health Insurance) Regulations, 2016, retaining
credit for accumulated waiting period coverage. Portability request must
be submitted to the new insurer at least 45 days before the renewal date.
The Company shall provide all required portability documentation within
5 working days of request.

Clause 9.4 – Cancellation by Insured
The Insured may cancel this Policy at any time by providing 15 days' written
notice. Refund of premium for the unexpired Policy period shall be provided
on a short-period rate basis:
- Up to 1 month risk: 75% of annual premium refunded
- Up to 3 months risk: 50% of annual premium refunded
- Up to 6 months risk: 25% of annual premium refunded
- Beyond 6 months risk: No refund
No refund shall be made if any claim has been admitted or paid during the
Policy Year.

Clause 9.5 – Cancellation by Company
The Company may cancel this Policy by providing 30 days' written notice in
cases of fraud, misrepresentation, non-disclosure of material facts, or moral
hazard. In such cases, premium shall be refunded on a pro-rata basis for the
unexpired period, except where fraud has been established.

SECTION 11: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q1. What is the minimum and maximum Sum Insured available under
    Swasthya Protect?
A1. Swasthya Protect is available at three Sum Insured options: ₹3 Lakh,
    ₹5 Lakh, and ₹10 Lakh. For higher Sum Insured options, please refer to
    Swasthya Protect Plus (SP002) or Swasthya Protect Global (SP003).

Q2. क्या मेरी पहले से मौजूद बीमारी (Pre-Existing Disease) इस पॉलिसी में
    कवर होगी?
    (Will my Pre-Existing Disease be covered under this Policy?)
A2. हाँ, लेकिन पहले से मौजूद बीमारियाँ पॉलिसी शुरू होने की तारीख से 48
    महीने की निरंतर कवरेज के बाद कवर की जाएंगी। यदि आपने पहले किसी अन्य
    बीमा कंपनी से स्वास्थ्य बीमा लिया हुआ है और उसे पोर्ट कर रहे हैं,
    तो IRDAI के नियमानुसार आपकी प्रतीक्षा अवधि में कमी हो सकती है।
    (Yes, Pre-Existing Diseases are covered after 48 months of continuous
    coverage from policy inception. If porting from another insurer, waiting
    period credit is available as per IRDAI portability guidelines.)

Q3. If my entire Sum Insured is exhausted in one hospitalisation, am
    I still covered for the rest of the year?
A3. Yes. Under Clause 4.12 (Restoration Benefit), the Company will reinstate
    100% of your Base Sum Insured once per Policy Year after it is fully
    exhausted. However, the restored amount cannot be used for the same
    illness or injury that caused the exhaustion.

Q4. Does the room rent limit affect other hospital charges?
A4. Yes. For Sum Insured of ₹3 Lakh and ₹5 Lakh, if you occupy a room with
    rent exceeding the applicable daily limit (₹2,500/day for ₹3L; ₹4,000/day
    for ₹5L), all associated hospital charges—including surgeon's fees,
    specialist fees, and nursing charges—will be proportionately reduced.
    To avoid this, we recommend selecting a room within the entitled category.
    There is no room rent restriction at the ₹10 Lakh Sum Insured level.

Q5. प्रीमियम का भुगतान करने के लिए कितने तरीके उपलब्ध हैं?
    (What modes of premium payment are available?)
A5. प्रीमियम का भुगतान नेट बैंकिंग, UPI, क्रेडिट/डेबिट कार्ड, NEFT/RTGS,
    या चेक के माध्यम से किया जा सकता है। वार्षिक, अर्धवार्षिक, और
    त्रैमासिक किश्तों में भुगतान का विकल्प उपलब्ध है।
    (Premium can be paid via net banking, UPI, credit/debit card, NEFT/RTGS,
    or cheque. Annual, half-yearly, and quarterly instalment options are
    available.)

Q6. How does No Claim Bonus work?
A6. For every claim-free Policy Year, your Sum Insured increases by 10% of
    the Base Sum Insured at renewal, at no extra premium. This bonus can
    accumulate up to a maximum of 50% of the Base Sum Insured. For example,
    if your Base Sum Insured is ₹5 Lakh and you have 5 claim-free years,
    your effective Sum Insured becomes ₹7.5 Lakh. If a claim occurs, the NCB
    reduces by 10% at the next renewal. NCB cannot increase the Sum Insured
    beyond 150% of the Base Sum Insured.

Q7. Can I add my parents to my Family Floater Policy?
A7. Parents and parents-in-law can be added to the Family Floater Policy.
    However, please note that their premium will be based on their individual
    age bands and may significantly increase the total premium. It may be more
    cost-effective to purchase a separate Senior Citizen plan (Swasthya Senior
    Shield SP006) for parents aged 60 and above. Please consult our advisor
    for a premium comparison.
