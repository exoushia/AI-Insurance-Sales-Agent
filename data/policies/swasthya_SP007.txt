SWASTHYA MATERNITY SURAKSHA
Health Insurance Policy – Standalone Maternity Plan
IRDAI UIN: SWIHLIP24007V012024
Policy Wording – Effective 01 April 2024

SECTION 1: PRODUCT OVERVIEW
Clause 1.1 – "Swasthya Maternity Suraksha" (SP007), UIN SWIHLIP24007V012024.
Clause 1.2 – Standalone maternity and newborn health insurance. Indemnity and
    benefit-based hybrid. Individual (woman) basis. May be combined with any
    other health policy.
Clause 1.3 – Designed for couples actively planning pregnancy or in early
    pregnancy stages. Covers: normal delivery, C-section, pre-natal, post-natal,
    newborn coverage for 90 days, fertility treatment (IVF up to ₹2 Lakh with
    3 attempts), and complications of pregnancy from Day 1.

SECTION 2: DEFINITIONS
Clause 2.1 – Accident: Sudden, unforeseen external event causing bodily injury.
Clause 2.2 – Cashless Facility (कैशलेस): Direct maternity hospital bill settlement.
Clause 2.3 – Claim (दावा): Formal demand for maternity benefit payment.
Clause 2.4 – Co-payment (सह-भुगतान): Not applicable.
Clause 2.5 – Deductible (कटौती): Not applicable.
Clause 2.6 – C-Section: Caesarean section delivery as advised by treating
    Obstetrician/Gynaecologist and documented in hospital records.
Clause 2.7 – Complications of Pregnancy: Obstetric complications requiring
    hospitalisation including: ectopic pregnancy, pre-eclampsia/eclampsia,
    placenta previa, abruptio placentae, threatened/inevitable/complete abortion
    requiring hospitalisation, antepartum haemorrhage, HELLP syndrome, and
    other life-threatening obstetric emergencies.
Clause 2.8 – Exclusion (बहिष्करण): Conditions/expenses excluded per Section 7.
Clause 2.9 – Fertility Treatment: Medically supervised procedures to assist
    conception including IVF (In Vitro Fertilisation), IUI (Intrauterine
    Insemination), ICSI, GIFT, ZIFT, cryopreservation of embryos, and
    hormonal stimulation cycles.
Clause 2.10 – Hospitalisation (अस्पताल में भर्ती): Admission for minimum 24
    hours for maternity care or complications, or as required for Day Care
    maternity procedures.
Clause 2.11 – Hospital: IRDAI-compliant registered hospital with obstetric
    and neonatal facilities.
Clause 2.12 – Insured Person: Female Proposer between 21 and 45 years.
Clause 2.13 – IVF Attempt: One complete IVF cycle including stimulation,
    egg retrieval, fertilisation, and embryo transfer.
Clause 2.14 – Network Hospital: Empanelled maternity hospital with cashless.
Clause 2.15 – Newborn: Child born to the Insured during the Policy Period.
Clause 2.16 – Normal Delivery: Vaginal delivery without surgical intervention.
Clause 2.17 – Pre-natal: Medical care received before delivery.
Clause 2.18 – Post-natal: Medical care received after delivery.
Clause 2.19 – Premium (प्रीमियम): Annual consideration per Policy Schedule, excl. GST.
Clause 2.20 – Waiting Period (प्रतीक्षा अवधि): 9-month waiting for delivery
    benefits (not for complication coverage).

SECTION 3: ELIGIBILITY AND ENROLLMENT
Clause 3.1 – Entry Age: Female Proposer: 21–45 years. No entry after 45 years.
Clause 3.2 – Eligible Insured: Individual female. May be purchased independently
    or alongside any other Swasthya health policy.
Clause 3.3 – Pregnancy Status at Entry: Insured may be non-pregnant or in
    early pregnancy (less than 12 weeks) at time of enrollment. If more than
    12 weeks pregnant at entry, normal delivery and C-section are not covered
    (complications remain covered if they arise after Policy inception date).
Clause 3.4 – Enrollment Documents: Proposal Form; Aadhaar/PAN (Proof of Identity);
    Age proof; Last menstrual period (LMP) date declaration; Obstetric history
    declaration; Bank account details.
Clause 3.5 – Free-Look Period: 15 days from Policy receipt.

SECTION 4: COVERAGE AND BENEFITS
Clause 4.1 – Normal Delivery Benefit
    Lump-sum benefit of ₹50,000 payable upon completion of normal delivery
    during Policy Period, subject to 9-month waiting period from Policy inception.
    Benefit paid regardless of actual hospital bill amount. No sub-limits.
    Includes: delivery room charges, midwife, nursing, anaesthesia (if epidural),
    immediate post-delivery care, lactation support.

Clause 4.2 – Caesarean Section Benefit
    Lump-sum benefit of ₹75,000 payable upon C-section delivery as medically
    advised. Subject to 9-month waiting period. Covers: surgeon, anaesthetist,
    OT charges, hospitalisation (up to 5 days), post-operative care.

Clause 4.3 – Pre-natal Expenses
    Reimbursement up to ₹10,000 for expenses incurred during the 9 months of
    pregnancy prior to delivery: antenatal check-ups (maximum 8 visits),
    prescribed ultrasounds (maximum 4 per pregnancy), blood tests, iron/folic
    acid supplementation (if prescribed). Requires valid prescriptions.

Clause 4.4 – Post-natal Expenses
    Reimbursement up to ₹10,000 for 60 days post-delivery: post-natal
    check-ups (maximum 4 visits), prescribed medications, physiotherapy,
    lactation consultant fees.

Clause 4.5 – Newborn Coverage (Day 1)
    Newborn child is automatically covered from birth for 90 days under this
    Policy without additional premium. Coverage includes: NICU charges if
    required (up to ₹1,00,000); neonatal hospitalisation for jaundice,
    respiratory distress, infection; congenital anomaly treatment (internal)
    if requiring immediate medical intervention; first 6 vaccinations per
    national immunisation schedule.
    After 90 days, the newborn must be enrolled in a separate health policy
    (SP001, SP002, or SP005 recommended).

Clause 4.6 – Complications of Pregnancy (No Waiting Period)
    Life-threatening complications of pregnancy are covered from Policy
    inception date (Day 1) with no waiting period, including:
    ectopic pregnancy; pre-eclampsia/eclampsia; antepartum haemorrhage;
    placenta previa; threatened/complete abortion requiring hospitalisation;
    HELLP syndrome; gestational diabetes requiring hospitalisation;
    postpartum haemorrhage; postpartum infections (uterus, urinary tract);
    emergency obstetric procedures. Covered up to available Sum Insured.

Clause 4.7 – Fertility Treatment (IVF/ART Benefit)
    Up to ₹2,00,000 per Policy Year for fertility treatment including:
    IVF cycles (maximum 3 attempts over Policy lifetime);
    IUI (unlimited within sub-limit); ICSI; embryo cryopreservation;
    hormonal stimulation (injectables); follicle monitoring ultrasounds;
    sperm analysis; pre-implantation genetic testing.
    Fertility treatment must be at ICMR-registered fertility centre.
    Requires: treating doctor's certificate of medical necessity;
    prior written approval from Company.
    Note: Fertility treatment waiting period: 6 months from Policy inception.

Clause 4.8 – Lawful Termination of Pregnancy
    Expenses for lawful termination of pregnancy (as per MTP Act, 1971)
    on medical grounds: covered up to ₹25,000. Not covered for elective
    termination without medical indication.

Clause 4.9 – Vaccination Schedule (Newborn under Clause 4.5)
    BCG, Hepatitis B (birth dose), OPV (birth dose), Pentavalent (3 doses),
    and Rotavirus (if prescribed). Total vaccination sub-limit: ₹5,000.

Clause 4.10 – Ambulance
    Road ambulance to maternity hospital: ₹2,000 per admission.

SECTION 5: SUM INSURED AND PREMIUM SCHEDULE
Clause 5.1 – Sum Insured Structure
    This Policy operates on a benefit basis for delivery (Clauses 4.1, 4.2)
    and on an indemnity basis for complications (Clause 4.6) and fertility
    (Clause 4.7). The total combined coverage is structured as:
    Plan A: Delivery ₹50K/₹75K + Complications up to ₹2L + Fertility ₹1L
    Plan B: Delivery ₹50K/₹75K + Complications up to ₹3L + Fertility ₹2L

Clause 5.2 – Annual Premium (Exclusive of GST):
    Age Band        Plan A      Plan B
    21–25 years    ₹12,500     ₹18,500
    26–30 years    ₹13,800     ₹20,200
    31–35 years    ₹16,500     ₹24,200
    36–40 years    ₹21,000     ₹30,800
    41–45 years    ₹28,500     ₹41,800

    Zone B: 10% discount. Multi-year: 2-year 7.5% discount.
    If purchased alongside SP001/SP002/SP003: 5% additional discount.

Clause 5.3 – Premium Factors: Age; Plan selection; Zone; obstetric history.
Clause 5.4 – GST: 18%. Section 80D deduction applies.
Clause 5.5 – Policy Limits: Delivery benefit payable maximum twice during
    lifetime of Policy (twin births count as one delivery).

SECTION 6: WAITING PERIODS
Clause 6.1 – Initial Waiting Period: 15 days for complications from Day 1
    (complications are covered from Day 1 actually — no waiting for life-
    threatening obstetric emergencies).
Clause 6.2 – Delivery Benefits Waiting Period: 9 months from Policy inception
    for normal delivery (Clause 4.1) and C-section (Clause 4.2) benefits.
    Pre-natal and post-natal covered from Day 1.
Clause 6.3 – Fertility Treatment Waiting Period: 6 months from Policy inception.
Clause 6.4 – PED: Obstetric history (e.g., prior C-section, PCOS) disclosed
    at proposal shall be covered subject to Company's medical underwriting
    assessment. May result in loading or specific waiting period.

Clause 7.6 – War, terrorism, nuclear risks.
Clause 7.7 – Infertility treatment beyond 3 IVF attempts per Policy lifetime.

Clause 7.15 – Routine antenatal tests beyond prescribed limits (Clause 4.3).

Clause 8.1 – Cashless: TPA/hospital intimate 48 hours before planned delivery;

Clause 8.2 – Delivery Benefit Claim: Submit within 30 days of delivery:
    Hospital birth certificate; discharge summary; itemised bill;
    completed claim form. Company pays lump-sum within 15 days.
Clause 8.3 – Complication Claims: Standard documents per Clause 8.4 SP001.
Clause 8.4 – Fertility Claims: Prior approval required. Submit: ICMR-registered
    centre letter; doctor's certificate of medical necessity; cycle details;
    itemised fertility centre invoice. Claim within 30 days of cycle completion.
Clause 8.5 – Newborn Claims: Newborn's hospital number; NICU records (if any);

Clause 8.6 – Settlement: 15 days for benefit claims; 30 days for indemnity claims.
Clause 8.7 – Investigation: 30 days.
Clause 8.8 – Fraud: Forfeiture; cancellation; IIB reporting.

SECTION 11: FAQs
Q1. I am 8 weeks pregnant. Can I still buy this Policy and be covered for delivery?
A1. Yes. You may enroll at up to 12 weeks of pregnancy. The 9-month delivery
    waiting period will mean your standard delivery coverage activates only after
    9 months from the Policy date. However, complications of pregnancy are covered
    from Day 1, which is critical protection for the remaining months of pregnancy.

Q2. क्या IVF ट्रीटमेंट पूरी तरह कवर है?
    (Is IVF treatment completely covered?)
A2. Plan B में ₹2,00,000 तक IVF और अन्य ART प्रक्रियाएं कवर हैं, पॉलिसी के
    जीवनकाल में अधिकतम 3 प्रयास के साथ। पूर्व अनुमोदन आवश्यक है और उपचार ICMR-
    पंजीकृत केंद्र में होना चाहिए। दवाइयाँ और मॉनिटरिंग टेस्ट भी शामिल हैं।
    (Plan B covers up to ₹2L for IVF and ART, maximum 3 attempts in Policy
    lifetime. Prior approval required; treatment at ICMR-registered centre.
    Includes medications and monitoring tests.)

Q3. Does my newborn need to be added to a separate policy?
A3. Your newborn is automatically covered for the first 90 days from birth under
    this Policy (NICU, hospitalisation, initial vaccinations). After 90 days,
    the child must be enrolled in a separate health policy. We recommend
    Swasthya Protect (SP001) or Swasthya Micro Bima (SP005) for newborns.

Q4. What is the delivery benefit for twins?
A4. Twin delivery is treated as one delivery event. The applicable benefit
    (₹50,000 for vaginal delivery, ₹75,000 for C-section) is paid once.
    Both twins are covered under the 90-day newborn coverage.

Q5. Is postpartum depression covered?
A5. Postpartum psychiatric conditions (postpartum depression, postpartum
    psychosis) requiring hospitalisation are covered under the complications
    benefit (Clause 4.6) with no waiting period. OPD psychiatric consultations
    are not covered under this Policy; refer to SP008 (Swasthya Women Wellness)
    for OPD mental health coverage.
