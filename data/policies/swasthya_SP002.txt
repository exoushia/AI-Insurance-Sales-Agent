SECTION 1: PRODUCT OVERVIEW
================================================================================

Clause 1.1 – Product Name and Identity
This Policy is issued under the product name "Swasthya Protect Plus"
(hereinafter referred to as "the Policy" or "SP002"), bearing IRDAI UIN
SWIHLIP24002V012024. It is offered by Swasthya Health Insurance Co. Limited
(hereinafter "the Company"), IRDAI Registration No. 147.

Clause 1.2 – Product Type and Structure
Swasthya Protect Plus is a comprehensive, indemnity-based health insurance
policy available on a Family Floater or Individual basis. It represents the
mid-tier offering in the Swasthya Protect product suite, positioned above
SP001 (Swasthya Protect) and below SP003 (Swasthya Protect Global). Key
enhancements over SP001 include: no room rent restrictions at all Sum
Insured levels; unlimited restoration for different illnesses; consumables
cover; and OPD benefit at the ₹10 Lakh and above tiers.

Clause 1.3 – Target Audience and Summary
Swasthya Protect Plus is designed for established families in metropolitan
and Tier 1 cities who require comprehensive health protection with greater
benefit depth. The Policy eliminates common sub-limit-related claim disputes
by offering no room rent restrictions and including consumables coverage. The
100% unlimited restoration benefit for different illnesses provides genuine
protection for families where multiple members may be hospitalised during
the same Policy Year.

SECTION 2: DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

Clause 2.1 – Accident
"Accident" means a sudden, unforeseen, and involuntary event caused by
external, visible, and violent means, resulting in Bodily Injury.

Clause 2.2 – Cashless Facility (कैशलेस)
"Cashless Facility" means the arrangement whereby the Company settles
hospital bills directly with the Network Hospital on behalf of the Insured
Person, to the extent of pre-authorisation approval and policy coverage.

Clause 2.3 – Claim (दावा)
"Claim" means a formal demand by the Insured or Insured Person for
indemnification of covered medical expenses under this Policy.

Clause 2.4 – Co-payment (सह-भुगतान)
"Co-payment" is not applicable under Swasthya Protect Plus unless a
voluntary co-payment option has been selected by the Insured at inception
(available as 10% co-payment with 12% premium discount or 20% co-payment
with 22% premium discount).

Clause 2.5 – Consumables Coverage
"Consumables" means medical items used during the course of hospitalisation
treatment that are ordinarily classified as non-payable under standard
insurance policies. Under Swasthya Protect Plus, all consumables as listed
in IRDAI's Circular No. IRDAI/HLT/CIR/MISC/151/08/2019 (including gloves,
syringes, gauze, surgical tape, nebuliser kits, and similar items) are
covered as a standard benefit.

Clause 2.6 – Deductible (कटौती)
"Deductible" means the amount the Insured must bear before the Company's
liability commences. No deductible applies under SP002 unless endorsed.

Clause 2.7 – Day Care Treatment
"Day Care Treatment" means any of the 570 approved procedures listed in
Annexure I that, owing to technological advancement, can be performed in
less than 24 hours but would otherwise have required In-patient Hospitalisation.

Clause 2.8 – Exclusion (बहिष्करण)
"Exclusion" means any condition, illness, treatment, or expense explicitly
excluded under Section 7 for which the Company bears no liability.

Clause 2.9 – Family
"Family" means: Self, legally wedded Spouse, dependent Children (natural,
adopted, or step-children aged 91 days to 25 years), dependent Parents, and
dependent Parents-in-Law. Maximum two adults and four dependent children per
Policy on Family Floater basis. Parents/parents-in-law may be included in
the same floater or added as separate members.

Clause 2.10 – Hospitalisation (अस्पताल में भर्ती)
"Hospitalisation" means admission in a duly registered Hospital for a minimum
of 24 consecutive hours for In-patient Care, except for listed Day Care
Treatments requiring less than 24 hours.

Clause 2.11 – Hospital
"Hospital" means any institution registered under the Clinical Establishments
(Registration and Regulation) Act, 2010, or meeting all minimum IRDAI-mandated
criteria as specified in the extant Health Insurance Regulations.

Clause 2.12 – Illness
"Illness" means any sickness, disease, or pathological condition requiring
medical treatment, including both acute and chronic conditions.

Clause 2.13 – Insured / Insured Person
"Insured" means the Proposer named in the Policy Schedule. "Insured Person"
means any member listed in the Policy Schedule for whom premium has been paid.

Clause 2.14 – Network Hospital
"Network Hospital" means a Hospital in the Company's approved panel as
updated at www.swasthyainsurance.in; 14,000+ hospitals across India as of
01 April 2024.

Clause 2.15 – No Claim Bonus (NCB)
"No Claim Bonus" means the automatic increase in Base Sum Insured upon
claim-free renewal, as per Clause 4.13.

Clause 2.16 – OPD Treatment
"OPD Treatment" means outpatient consultation, diagnosis, or treatment at
a clinic or hospital without admission as a Day Care or In-patient.

Clause 2.17 – Pre-Existing Disease (पहले से मौजूद बीमारी)
"Pre-Existing Disease" means any condition diagnosed or treated within 48
months prior to the first Policy commencement date with the Company.

Clause 2.18 – Premium (प्रीमियम)
"Premium" means the consideration paid by the Insured for insurance coverage,
as stated in the Policy Schedule, exclusive of GST.

Clause 2.19 – Sum Insured (बीमा राशि)
"Sum Insured" means the maximum aggregate Company liability for all covered
claims during the Policy Year per Policy Schedule.

Clause 2.20 – Waiting Period (प्रतीक्षा अवधि)
"Waiting Period" means the duration from Policy inception during which
specified diseases, conditions, or treatments are not covered.

SECTION 4: COVERAGE AND BENEFITS
================================================================================

Clause 4.1 – In-patient Hospitalisation
Full indemnity for medically necessary In-patient treatment covering:
room/boarding/nursing charges without room rent cap; ICU/ICCU charges
without cap; surgeon, anaesthetist, specialist fees; operation theatre
charges; blood, oxygen, diagnostics; medicines and consumables
(including IRDAI non-payable list items as per Clause 4.6).

Clause 4.2 – No Room Rent Sub-Limit
Unlike SP001, Swasthya Protect Plus carries no room rent or ICU sub-limit
at any Sum Insured level. The Insured Person is entitled to any room category
(including single private AC room). All associated charges (surgeon fees,
nursing fees) are payable at actual rates applicable to the chosen room.

Clause 4.3 – Pre-Hospitalisation Expenses
Covered for 60 days prior to date of Hospitalisation for the same condition.

Clause 4.4 – Post-Hospitalisation Expenses
Covered for 180 days following date of discharge for the same condition.
This extended 180-day post-hospitalisation benefit (vs 90 days in SP001)
is particularly beneficial for recovery from major surgeries and critical
illness episodes.

Clause 4.5 – Day Care Treatments
570 Day Care procedures covered (Annexure I). Pre-authorisation required.

Clause 4.6 – Consumables Coverage
All consumables as per IRDAI's published non-payable list are covered under
this Policy, including: gloves, syringes, gauze, cotton, surgical tape, masks,
nebuliser kits, urometer, vasofix, etc. This eliminates common out-of-pocket
expenses for Insured Persons during hospitalisation.

Clause 4.7 – Domiciliary Hospitalisation
Covered up to 25% of Sum Insured per Policy Year, for conditions where
hospitalisation would normally be required but home treatment was medically
advised. Standard Domiciliary exclusions (asthma, hypertension, diabetes
in isolation) apply per IRDAI guidelines.

Clause 4.8 – OPD Benefit (₹10 Lakh and ₹25 Lakh/₹50 Lakh SI)
At Sum Insured of ₹10 Lakh: OPD benefit of ₹15,000 per Policy Year covers
outpatient consultations, prescribed diagnostics, and pharmacy (excluding
chronic maintenance medications that are non-hospitalisation-related).
At Sum Insured of ₹25 Lakh and ₹50 Lakh: OPD benefit of ₹25,000 per
Policy Year. OPD benefit is not available at ₹5 Lakh SI.
No sub-limit per consultation; pharmacy bills must be supported by prescription.
OPD benefit cannot be used in conjunction with a reimbursement claim for the
same consultation.

Clause 4.9 – Ambulance Charges
Emergency road ambulance: ₹3,000 per Hospitalisation. Air ambulance: Not
covered under SP002; available under SP003.

Clause 4.10 – AYUSH Treatment
In-patient AYUSH treatment (Ayurveda, Yoga, Naturopathy, Unani, Siddha,
Homeopathy) covered up to 50% of Sum Insured per Policy Year at recognised
AYUSH hospitals. Higher AYUSH sub-limit vs SP001's 25%.

Clause 4.11 – Organ Donor Expenses
Covered up to full available Sum Insured including consumables.

Clause 4.12 – Mental Health Coverage
In-patient psychiatric treatment covered up to ₹3,00,000 per Policy Year.
OPD psychiatric consultations: up to ₹10,000 per Policy Year (from OPD
benefit at ₹10L+ SI).

Clause 4.13 – Restoration Benefit (Unlimited – Different Illnesses)
Upon exhaustion of the entire Sum Insured during a Policy Year, the Company
shall restore 100% of the Base Sum Insured. Unlike SP001's single restoration,
Swasthya Protect Plus provides unlimited restorations per Policy Year, each
for illnesses or injuries different from those that caused the prior
exhaustion. The Restored Sum Insured: (a) is available only for different
illnesses/injuries; (b) cannot be used for the same illness that caused
exhaustion; (c) is not carried forward; (d) NCB does not apply on restored
amounts.
Example: Family floater ₹10L. Member A is hospitalised for cardiac event
(claim ₹10L – SI exhausted). Restoration triggers. Member B is subsequently
hospitalised for an unrelated accident – full ₹10L restored SI is available.

Clause 4.14 – No Claim Bonus (NCB)
10% of Base Sum Insured per claim-free year. Maximum NCB: 50% of Base SI.
NCB reduction: 10% per year with claim. NCB cannot reduce below zero.
At ₹10L SI with 5 claim-free years: effective SI = ₹15L.

Clause 4.15 – Maternity Benefit (Rider)
Available as optional rider at ₹10L and above SI. Normal delivery: ₹50,000;
C-section: ₹75,000; pre-natal expenses: ₹10,000; post-natal: ₹10,000;
Newborn cover from Day 1 for 90 days. 9-month waiting period applies.
Refer to Maternity Rider document MR-002 for complete terms.

Clause 8.7 – Claim for Consumables

SECTION 11: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q1. How does Swasthya Protect Plus differ from Swasthya Protect (SP001)?
A1. The key differences are: (1) No room rent restrictions at any SI level
    (SP001 has caps at ₹3L and ₹5L); (2) Consumables coverage included
    (not in SP001); (3) Unlimited restoration for different illnesses (SP001
    offers single restoration); (4) OPD benefit at ₹10L+ SI (not in SP001);
    (5) Extended post-hospitalisation cover of 180 days (vs 90 in SP001);
    (6) Higher AYUSH sub-limit (50% vs 25%); (7) Higher Network Hospital count
    (14,000 vs 12,000). Premium is approximately 26–30% higher at same SI.

Q2. क्या कंज्यूमेबल्स कवरेज का मतलब है कि अस्पताल की पूरी बिल माफ हो जाएगी?
    (Does consumables coverage mean the entire hospital bill is waived?)
A2. नहीं, कंज्यूमेबल्स कवरेज का मतलब है कि IRDAI की नॉन-पेयेबल लिस्ट में
    दर्ज आइटम जैसे ग्लव्स, सिरिंज, गॉज, सर्जिकल टेप आदि की लागत आपकी
    बीमा कंपनी वहन करेगी। अन्य गैर-कवर्ड आइटम अभी भी आपकी जिम्मेदारी होंगे।
    (No, consumables coverage means items on IRDAI's non-payable list like
    gloves, syringes, gauze, and surgical tape are covered. Other non-covered
    items remain the Insured's responsibility.)

Q3. The restoration benefit says 'different illness.' What does that mean?
A3. If the Sum Insured is exhausted due to, say, a cardiac hospitalisation
    for Member A, the restored amount cannot be used if Member A is readmitted
    for the same cardiac condition. However, if Member B (on the same Family
    Floater) is hospitalised for a completely unrelated condition such as a
    fracture or typhoid, the full restored Sum Insured is available for them.
    Each restoration is for an illness different from the one that caused
    the prior exhaustion.

Q4. Can I use the OPD benefit for medicines for a chronic condition like
    hypertension?
A4. Yes, pharmacy bills for medications prescribed by a registered Medical
    Practitioner, including maintenance medications for chronic conditions,
    are covered under the OPD benefit at ₹10L and above SI. Bills must be
    accompanied by a valid prescription not older than 60 days. The annual
    OPD limit is ₹15,000 (₹10L SI) or ₹25,000 (₹25L/₹50L SI).

Q5. Is there a specific limit for ICU charges?
A5. No. Swasthya Protect Plus has no room rent or ICU sub-limits at any Sum
    Insured level. Actual ICU charges are payable subject to the overall Sum
    Insured and reasonable and customary charge standards.

Q6. How does the voluntary co-payment option work?
A6. If you opt for a 10% voluntary co-payment at inception, you will bear 10%
    of every admissible claim, and receive a 12% discount on annual premium.
    With 20% co-payment, you receive a 22% premium discount but bear 20% of
    every claim. Co-payment selection cannot be changed mid-policy; it takes
    effect from the next renewal.

Q7. What is the claims settlement ratio for Swasthya Health Insurance?
A7. Swasthya Health Insurance Co. maintains a Claim Settlement Ratio (CSR) of
    96.8% for SP002, reflecting 96.8 claims paid per 100 claims received.
    This ratio is audited and published annually in the IRDAI Annual Report.
