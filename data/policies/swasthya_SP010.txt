PRODUCT PHILOSOPHY
Swasthya Super Top-Up is designed as an intelligent financial layer above your existing health
insurance. Unlike a simple top-up (which activates per hospitalisation), Super Top-Up operates
on an AGGREGATE DEDUCTIBLE basis — meaning the deductible threshold is measured across ALL
hospitalisations in a policy year, not per event. This makes it significantly more cost-efficient
for families with multiple or prolonged hospitalisations.

Hindi: यह पॉलिसी "संचित कटौती" (Sanchit Katoti) के आधार पर काम करती है।

CLAUSE 1: DEFINITIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1.1 AGGREGATE DEDUCTIBLE (संचित कटौती): The cumulative total of all admissible medical
    expenses paid by the Insured or their primary policy during a Policy Year, beyond which
    this policy begins to pay. The Aggregate Deductible is NOT reset per hospitalisation —
    it accumulates across the entire policy year.

1.2 PRIMARY POLICY: Any existing health insurance policy, employer group cover, government
    scheme (PM-JAY, CGHS, ECHS), or personal savings that the Insured uses to fund expenses
    up to the deductible. Swasthya Super Top-Up does not require the Insured to hold a
    specific primary policy.

1.3 DEDUCTIBLE EXHAUSTION DATE: The date in a Policy Year on which cumulative admissible
    expenses first equal or exceed the Aggregate Deductible amount.

1.4 POLICY YEAR: 12 consecutive months from the Policy Commencement Date. Aggregate
    Deductible resets to zero at the start of each new Policy Year.

1.5 SUM INSURED (बीमित राशि): The maximum amount payable by this policy in a Policy Year,
    above the Aggregate Deductible.

1.6 HOSPITALISATION: Admission to a registered hospital for a minimum period of 24 consecutive
    hours for medical treatment, except for specified day-care procedures.

CLAUSE 3: HOW AGGREGATE DEDUCTIBLE WORKS — WORKED EXAMPLES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The following examples assume STU-Gold (₹50L SI) with ₹5L Aggregate Deductible.
Primary policy: SP001 Swasthya Protect (₹5L SI).

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EXAMPLE A: SINGLE LARGE HOSPITALISATION — ₹8 LAKH BILL
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Scenario: Mr. Sharma (age 42) undergoes cardiac bypass surgery. Total bill: ₹8,00,000.

  Step 1 — Primary policy (SP001) pays: ₹5,00,000 (full SI exhausted)
  Step 2 — Aggregate Deductible status: ₹5,00,000 accumulated = Deductible EXHAUSTED
  Step 3 — SP010 Super Top-Up pays: ₹3,00,000 (balance of ₹8L bill)
  Step 4 — Out-of-pocket: ₹0

  Summary:
    Hospital Bill          : ₹8,00,000
    Primary policy paid    : ₹5,00,000
    SP010 paid             : ₹3,00,000
    Insured's expense      : ₹0

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EXAMPLE B: MULTIPLE SMALLER HOSPITALISATIONS — ₹15 LAKH TOTAL
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Scenario: Mrs. Sharma has three hospitalisations in one policy year.
    Event 1 (January): Appendectomy — Bill ₹2,80,000
    Event 2 (April):   Knee surgery — Bill ₹4,20,000
    Event 3 (October): Dengue (ICU) — Bill ₹8,00,000

  SIMPLE TOP-UP (per-event deductible, shown for comparison):
    Event 1: Bill ₹2.8L < ₹5L deductible → SP010 pays ₹0 | Out-of-pocket: ₹2.8L
    Event 2: Bill ₹4.2L < ₹5L deductible → SP010 pays ₹0 | Out-of-pocket: ₹4.2L
    Event 3: Primary pays ₹5L; SP010 pays ₹3L | Out-of-pocket: ₹0
    Total out-of-pocket with Simple Top-Up: ₹7,00,000

  AGGREGATE TOP-UP (SP010 — how it actually works):
    Event 1: Bill ₹2.8L — Primary (SP001 restored) pays ₹2.8L
             Aggregate accumulated: ₹2,80,000
    Event 2: Bill ₹4.2L — Primary pays ₹2.2L (uses remaining ₹2.2L of restored SI)
             Aggregate accumulated: ₹2,80,000 + ₹2,20,000 = ₹5,00,000 → EXHAUSTED
             Remaining ₹2L of Event 2 bill → SP010 pays ₹2,00,000
    Event 3: Bill ₹8L — Primary SI exhausted. SP010 pays ₹8,00,000 (within ₹50L SI)
    Total out-of-pocket with SP010 Aggregate: ₹0

  SAVING vs. SIMPLE TOP-UP: ₹7,00,000

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EXAMPLE C: CATASTROPHIC HOSPITALISATION — ₹30 LAKH BILL (ICU + ORGAN TRANSPLANT)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Scenario: Mr. Verma (age 50) — liver transplant + 45-day ICU stay. Total: ₹30,00,000.

    Primary policy (SP002 ₹10L SI) pays     : ₹10,00,000
    Aggregate Deductible status             : ₹10L > ₹5L → EXHAUSTED in this event
    SP010-Platinum (₹1Cr, ₹10L deductible) pays:
      (₹10L primary already covers deductible of ₹10L → SP010 pays ₹20,00,000)
    Out-of-pocket                           : ₹0

    Total bill: ₹30,00,000 | Insured pays: ₹0

    a) Original/attested discharge summaries and itemised bills for ALL hospitalisations
       in the policy year contributing to the deductible
    b) Settlement receipts/letters from primary policy or proof of personal payment
    c) Bank statements or payment receipts if self-funded

7.2 DEDUCTIBLE TRACKER: The Swasthya mobile app provides a real-time Aggregate
    Deductible Tracker. Insured may upload expense proofs progressively through the year.
    Once the tracker confirms deductible exhaustion, a "Deductible Exhaustion Certificate"
    is issued digitally — this simplifies subsequent claims in the same policy year.

7.3 CLAIM INTIMATION: Within 48 hours of hospitalisation (planned) or 24 hours
    (emergency). Claims must be filed within 30 days of discharge.

7.4 SETTLEMENT TAT: Cashless — 2 hours pre-auth; Reimbursement — 15 working days
    from receipt of complete documents.
