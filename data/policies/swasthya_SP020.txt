PRODUCT PHILOSOPHY
India's joint family is not just a social unit — it is an economic unit where health
expenditure can cascade across three generations simultaneously. An elderly parent's
surgery, a working-age child's cardiac event, and a grandchild's accident can each
strike in the same year.

SP020 Family Legacy is India's first three-generation health insurance — a single
policy covering up to 8 family members across grandparents, parents, and children.
It combines INDIVIDUAL SUM INSURED (each member has their own SI) with a SHARED
FAMILY POOL (an additional buffer accessible by any member after individual SI is
exhausted) — giving each family member financial independence while preserving a
collective safety net.

Hindi: "पारिवारिक विरासत बीमा" (Parivarik Virasat Bima) — तीन पीढ़ियों की एक साथ
सुरक्षा — व्यक्तिगत बीमा + साझा परिवार कोष।

1.1 PROPOSER (प्रस्तावक): The primary applicant who applies for and manages this policy.
    Must be aged 25–55 years at inception.

1.2 ELIGIBLE FAMILY MEMBERS: Any combination of up to 8 members from the following
    three generations:
    Generation 1 (G1): Proposer's parents and/or parents-in-law (maximum 4 members)
    Generation 2 (G2): Proposer and/or spouse (maximum 2 members)
    Generation 3 (G3): Proposer's dependent children (maximum 4 members, up to age 25)

    Minimum composition: At least 2 members from at least 2 different generations.

1.3 INDIVIDUAL SUM INSURED (ISI / व्यक्तिगत बीमित राशि): A dedicated sum insured
    assigned to each enrolled member, specified in the Policy Schedule.

1.4 SHARED FAMILY POOL (SFP / साझा पारिवारिक कोष): An additional combined buffer
    available to ALL enrolled members after their individual ISI is exhausted in a policy
    year. The SFP is replenished in full at the start of each new policy year.

1.5 AUTO SI INCREASE (ASI): An automatic annual increase in ISI for each member, without
    fresh underwriting, at a specified percentage, subject to the caps in Clause 3.3.

1.6 GENERATION PREMIUM WEIGHTING: Each generation attracts a different premium rate per
    rupee of ISI, reflecting age and risk profile.

2.2 HOW THE TWO LAYERS WORK (Illustration):

    Scenario: Mrs. Sharma (G1, age 68) has a hospitalisation costing ₹9 Lakhs.
    Mrs. Sharma's ISI (FL-Comfort) = ₹5 Lakhs.
    — Step 1: ISI pays ₹5,00,000 (exhausted).
    — Step 2: Shared Pool (₹25L) pays the remaining ₹4,00,000.
    — Step 3: Shared Pool remaining balance = ₹21,00,000 (available to any member).
    — Mrs. Sharma's ISI restored at next year's renewal to ₹5 Lakhs.
    — Other family members' ISIs are UNAFFECTED.

    Key contrast with a simple floater: In a floater, Mrs. Sharma's ₹9L claim would
    have consumed ₹9L from a shared SI, leaving little for others. With FL-Comfort,
    each member is ring-fenced until their ISI is exhausted.

CLAUSE 3: AUTO SUM INSURED INCREASE (ASI)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 AUTOMATIC INCREASE: At each annual renewal, the ISI of every member automatically
    increases by 5% — without fresh medical examination, without underwriting review.

3.2 INFLATION PROTECTION PURPOSE: Medical inflation in India runs at 8–12% per annum.
    ASI ensures the ISI does not lose real purchasing power over the policy's lifetime.

3.3 AUTO INCREASE CAPS (to prevent unlimited accumulation):

    Member Generation | Maximum ISI Cap (ASI stops at)
    ------------------|------------------------------
    G3 (Children)     | ₹25 Lakhs (ISI); transitions to independent policy at age 25
    G2 (Working-age)  | ₹1 Crore (ISI)
    G1 (Parents/seniors)| ₹50 Lakhs (ISI)

3.4 ENHANCED AUTO INCREASE: Proposer may opt for a 10% ASI (at +₹2,000/year additional
    premium) to match higher medical inflation scenarios.

3.5 CLAIM-YEAR SUSPENSION: In any year where the individual ISI is fully claimed, the
    ASI does not increase that member's ISI the following year (frozen at current level
    for one year; resumes thereafter).
